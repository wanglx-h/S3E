Subject: Tongue-twister
A '''tongue-twister''' is a phrase that is hard to speech. They often use alliteration and homophones. They are commonly employed to help warm up Singer and Actor Tongue-twisters can range from simple phrases for beginners to complex sequences that require advanced vocal control. They are popular in language learning, helping non-native speakers improve their pronunciation and familiarity with tricky sounds. Additionally, tongue-twisters are often used in competitions or as games, where participants try to say them without stumbling == Examples == * How much wood would a woodchuck chuck if a woodchuck could chuck wood? He would chuck as many wood as a woodchuck chucks if a woodchuck could chuck wood. * She sells sea-shells on the sea-shore. The sea shells she sells are sea shells for sure. * Six sick hicks nick six slick bricks with picks and sticks. * The lips, the teeth, the tip of the tongue. == References == Category:Linguistics
