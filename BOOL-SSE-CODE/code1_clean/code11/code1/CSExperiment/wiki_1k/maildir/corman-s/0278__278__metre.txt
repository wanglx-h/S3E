File:5 metres (8349446738) (2).jpg A '''metre''' (US spelling, ''meter'') is the basic units of measurement of length in the SI measurement system. The symbol for the metre is '''m'''. The first meaning (in the French Revolution) was one ten-millionth of the distance between the Earth's equator and the North Pole along the Paris meridian. The metre is now defined as the distance light travels in a vacuum in 1/299,792,458 of a second. In the Imperial unit system of measurement, one yard is 0.9144 metres (after international agreement in 1959), so a metre is very near to 39.37 inches: about 3.281 Foot (unit of length), or 1.0936 US yard.
