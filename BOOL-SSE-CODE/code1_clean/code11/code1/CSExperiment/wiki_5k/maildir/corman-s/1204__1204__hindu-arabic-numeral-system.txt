Subject: Hindu–Arabic numeral system
The '''Hindu–Arabic numeral system''', sometimes known as the '''Indo–Arabic numeral''' or '''Latin numeral system''' or simply the '''Hindu numeral system''', '''Indo numeral system''' or '''Arabic numeral system''', is a Numeral system used all around the world.Flegg, Graham 2002. ''Numbers: their history and meaning''. Courier Dover. It is a Decimal Place value system. This system has ten basic lowercase-only symbols, which are 0 (number), 1 (number), 2 (number), 3 (number), 4 (number), 5 (number), 6 (number), 7 (number), 8 (number) and 9 (number). The Hindu–Arabic numeral system was first created by the India in the 6th century and used by the Arab people in the 7th century and Roman people in the 16th century. In Europe, Hindu-Arabic numeral system became very popular after the publication of ''Liber Abaci,'' a book written by Italy mathematician Fibonacci. They are very different from previous methods of counting, such as the abacus. The numbers allowed mathematics to develop. In the past, many other number systems had been used.Menninger, Karl 1969.
