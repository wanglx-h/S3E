Subject: Lincoln Thompson
'''Lincoln Thompson''' (10 July 1949 - 14 January 1999) was a Jamaican reggae musician, songwriter and Rastafarian. He spent 2 years in the Tartans as a teenager before working under Coxsone Dodd in the early 1970s. In 1974, he released his first album, ''Humanity''. The album had popular single (music) but it did not sell many copies until Thompson made a deal with Ballistics Records, a part of the record label United Artists, in 1979. This album was released commerce and there were 2 other albums recorded with Ballistics. These albums were recording in 1979 and 1980. ''Experience'' was recorded in Jamaica. The next album, ''Natural Wild'', was recorded in England with Joe Jackson. Ballistics went bankrupt, and Thompson returned to Jamaica. There, he recorded the album, ''Ride with the Rasses'' in 1982. The next year, he took his family to live in London where he recorded a 5th album, ''Rootsman Blues''. He then set up an Rastafarian Grocery store named ''The Rasses Fish and Grocery Store'' in London. Thanks to an United States sponsorship, a 6th album came out in 1996, called ''21st century''.
