File:Human artifacts at CMNH - 37.JPG]] '''Polytheism''' means believing in many Deity. A person that believes in polytheism is called a '''polytheist'''. A religion with polytheism can be called a '''polytheistic religion'''. Polytheism is well documented in historical religions of classical antiquity, especially those of Ancient Greece and Ancient Rome. Other Ancient history peoples who were polytheists were Germanic paganism, Turkic peoples, Ancient Egyptians, Celts, and the Norse. There are various polytheistic religions that are practiced today such as Sanamahism (Meitei paganism), Tengrism, Shinto, Chinese folk religion, Wicca, Druidism, Taoism, Asatru and Candombl√©. Polytheistic religions usually include a set of story about the gods, which is called mythology.
