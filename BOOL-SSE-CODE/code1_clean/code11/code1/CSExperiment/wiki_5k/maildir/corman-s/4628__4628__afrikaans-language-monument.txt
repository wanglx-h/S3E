File:AfrikaanseTaalmonumentObelisks.jpg, Western Cape, South Africa.]] The '''Afrikaans Language Monument''' () is the only monument in the world dedicated to a language. It is on a hill overlooking the Teachers' Training College in Paarl, Western Cape Province, South Africa. Completed in 1975, it was built to celebrate the 100 years birthday of Afrikaans being declared as a different language from Dutch language. The monument is made of tapering structures of a convex and concave nature. They show the influences of different languages and cultures on Afrikaans itself. They are also a symbol of Politics developments in South Africa, as follows: * Clear West - the European source of the language * Magical Africa - the African influences on the language * Bridge - between Europe and Africa * Afrikaans - the language itself * Republic - declared in 1961 * Malay language and culture File:AfrikaanseTaalmonumentEnscriptions.jpg == Inscriptions == On a large inscription at the entrance, two quotes from important Afrikaans poets are written. === van Wyk Louw inscription === * '''' -- N.P. van Wyk Louw : "Afrikaans is the language that connects Western Europe and Africa...
