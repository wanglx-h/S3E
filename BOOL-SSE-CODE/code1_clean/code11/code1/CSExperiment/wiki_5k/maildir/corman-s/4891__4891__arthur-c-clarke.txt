|birth_place=Minehead, Somerset, England|death_date=|death_place=Colombo, Sri Lanka|occupation=|alma_mater=King's College London|spouse=|period=1946–2008|genre=Science fictionPopular science|subject=Science|notableworks=''2001: A Space Odyssey''|website=|honorific prefix=Sir|honorific suffix=}}Sir '''Arthur Charles Clarke''' (Minehead, Somerset, 6 December 1917 – Colombo, Sri Lanka, 19 March 2008) was a British people writer and inventor. He was most famous for his science fiction novel ''2001: A Space Odyssey'', and for working with movie director Stanley Kubrick on the movie of the same name. Clarke and Isaac Asimov were probably the two best-known science fiction writers of their day. Some of Clarke's novels include ''Childhood's End'', ''A Fall of Moondust'', ''The Songs of Distant Earth'', ''The Sands of Mars'', and ''Meeting with Medusa''. He also wrote many short story, and several serious works on science.Clarke, Arthur C. 1950. ''Interplanetary flight: an introduction to astronautics''. London: Temple Press.Clarke, Arthur C. 1951. ''The exploration of space''. London: Temple Press, 1951. Updated/revised 1959 and 1979 (with a new introduction).Clarke, Arthur C. 1962. ''Profiles of the future''. London: Gollancz, and Pan Books. Clarke served in the Royal Air Force as a radar instructor and technician from 1941 to 1946. He proposed a satellite (artificial) communication system in 1945 which won him the Franklin Institute Gold Medal in 1963.[http://lakdiva.org/clarke/1945ww The 1945 Proposal by Arthur C. Clarke for Geostationary Satellite Communications] He was the chairman of the British Interplanetary Society from 1947 to 1950 and again from 1951 to 1953. Clarke emigrated to Sri Lanka in 1956 largely to pursue his interest in scuba diving; That year, he discovered the underwater ruins of the ancient Koneswaram temple in Trincomalee. He lived in Sri Lanka until his death. He was Knight Bachelor by Queen Elizabeth II in 1998, and was awarded Sri Lanka's highest civil honour, Sri Lankabhimanya, in 2005.[http://www.priu.gov.lk/news_update/Current_Affairs/ca200511/National_Honours_Gazette_Notification.pdf Government Notification—National Honours] , November 2005.
