| birth_place = San Diego, California, United States | occupation = Actor, director | years_active = 1952–present | spouse = Barbara Benjamin (1964–1975)Gail Youngs (1982–1986)Sharon Brophy (1991–1996)Luciana Pedraza (2005–present) }} '''Robert Selden Duvall''' (born January 5, 1931) is an Americans actor and director. Duvall won an Academy Award for Best Actor for his role in ''Tender Mercies'' (1983). He has also won the Screen Actors Guild Award, several Golden Globe Awards, and a BAFTA Award. == Early life == Duvall was born in San Diego, California. His parents were Mildred Virginia Hart and William Howard Duvall. He studied at Severn School in Severna Park, Maryland and The Principia in St. Louis, Missouri. He graduated, in 1953, from Principia College in Elsah, Illinois. == Career == Duvall's career began in 1952. His first role was as Arthur "Boo" Radley in the 1962 movie ''To Kill A Mockingbird (movie)''. He then appeared on ''Captain Newman, M.D.'' (1963). He landed many of his most famous roles during the early 1970s, such as Major Frank Burns in the blockbuster comedy ''MASH (movie)'' (1970) and the lead role in ''THX 1138'' (1971), as well as Horton Foote's adaptation of William Faulkner's ''Tomorrow (1972 movie)'' (1972). This was followed by a series of critically lauded performances in commercially successful films. == Personal life == Duvall's political views are variously described as Libertarianism or Conservatism in the United States. Duvall was married to Barbara Benjamin from 1964 until they divorced in 1975. Then, he was married to Gail Youngs from 1982 through 1985. Then he was married to Sharon Brophy from 1991 through 1996. He has been married to Argentine actress Luciana Duvall since 2005. Duvall has no children. He lives in The Plains, Virginia. == Movies == He has appeared in many other films including: * ''To Kill A Mockingbird'' (1962) * ''M*A*S*H'' (1970) * ''The Godfather'' (1972) * ''Tomorrow (movie)'' (1972) * ''Network (1976 movie)'' (1976) * ''Apocalypse Now'' (1979) * ''True Confessions (movie)'' (1981) * ''Colors (movie)'' (1988) * Sling Blade (1996) * ''The Apostle'' (1997) * ''Deep Impact (movie)'' (1998) * ''The Judge (2014 movie)'' (2014) * ''Widows (2018 movie)'' (2018) * Days of thunder (1990) == Legacy == In 2015, at age 84, Duvall became the oldest person ever nominated for Best Supporting Actor at the Academy Awards, with a nomination for the movie ''The Judge (2014 movie).'' == References == == Other websites == * * Category:American Academy Award winning actors Category:Golden Globe Award winning actors Category:BAFTA Award winning actors Category:Emmy Award winning actors Category:Screen Actors Guild Award winners Category:Actors from San Diego Category:Actors from Virginia Category:Actors from Maryland Category:Actors from Illinois Category:Actors from St. Louis, Missouri Category:Movie directors from California Category:Movie directors from Illinois Category:Republicans (United States) Category:American movie actors Category:American television actors Category:American stage actors Category:American voice actors Category:American activists Category:1931 births Category:Living people
