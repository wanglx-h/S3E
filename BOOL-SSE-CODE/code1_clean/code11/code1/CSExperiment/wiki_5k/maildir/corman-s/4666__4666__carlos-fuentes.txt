Subject: Carlos Fuentes
File:Carlos Fuentes 1987.jpg '''Carlos Fuentes''' (11 November 1928 – 15 May 2012) was a Mexico novelist, essayist, screenwriter, and diplomat. Fuentes was born in Panama City, Panama. His father was a Mexican diplomat, and while Fuentes was growing up, the family moved to different countries in South America and then to the United States. He got a Academic degree in law from the National Autonomous University of Mexico, but instead of being a lawyer, he chose to be a diplomat and writer. He published his first novel, called ''La región más transparente'' (''Where the Air Is Clear'' in English) in 1958. From 1975 to 1977, Fuentes was Mexico's ambassador to France.
