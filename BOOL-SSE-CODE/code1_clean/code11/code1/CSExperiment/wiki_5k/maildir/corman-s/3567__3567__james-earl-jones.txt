| birth_place = Arkabutla, Mississippi, Mississippi, U.S. | death_date = | death_place = Pawling (town), New York, New York (state), U.S. | occupation = Actor | spouse = Julienne Marie (1968–1972; divorced)Cecilia Hart (1982–2016; her death) | children = 1 | father = Robert Earl Jones | yearsactive = 1951–2021 | website = }} '''James Earl Jones''' (January 17, 1931 September 9, 2024) was an Americans actor. He was famous for doing the voice of Darth Vader in the ''Star Wars'' movies, for doing the voice of Mufasa in ''The Lion King'' and in the The Lion King (2019 movie), and for the CNN tagline ("This...is CNN"). He starred in ''Lincoln Portrait'' which has music by Aaron Copland. Jones won two Tony Awards for his live theater work on Broadway. He won an Emmy Award for his role on the television series ''Gabriel's Fire''. In 1991, he became the first actor to win two Emmys in the same year. He was a son of actor Robert Earl Jones. == Death == Jones died at his home in Pawling (town), New York, New York (state) on September 9, 2024. He was 93 years old.
