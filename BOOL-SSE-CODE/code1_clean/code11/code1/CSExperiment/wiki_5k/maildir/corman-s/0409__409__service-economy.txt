Subject: Service economy
A '''service''' is a job or work done for someone else. All the service trades form a '''service economy'''. Good examples are: *Government is perhaps the largest example. *Hospital and the health services generally. *Bank and financial services. *Engine trade services. All services except actual products such as parts and petrol (which are obviously goods or products). *Painting a house as opposed to building it. That has been so at least for the past 40 years.Bauer, Roy A. and others. 1992. The Silverlake Project: transformation at IBM (AS/400). Oxford University Press. The person or company which gives the service will get something in return for the service, obviously. Who gives the service usually gets money in return. Who gives the service may get goods in return. Who gives the service can get another service in return. This is a type of trade. == References == Category:Economics
