It is west of London, and southeast of Bristol. Bath is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.UNESCO, [http://whc.unesco.org/en/list/428 "City of Bath"]; retrieved 2012-4-19. The city has preserved some of its Roman Empire remains and its 18th century architecture. ==History== The city gets its name from the famous Ancient Rome baths in the town. The Romans built the baths as part of a spa, in the year 43BC. They called it ''Aquae Sulis'', which means "The waters of Sulis". Sulis was a local goddess. During the Middle Ages, it was an important city for buying and selling wool. Bath became a city in 1585, when Queen Elizabeth I declared it to be one. Bath has two universities and several schools and colleges. Bath is where Roald Dahl's short story "The Landlady" takes place.
