'''Syracuse''' is a city in the U.S. state of New York (state). It is also the county seat of Onondaga County, New York. Syracuse has a population of 148,620 and a metropolitan area population of 662,000. Syracuse is sometimes called the "Salt City" because salt mining was once the main industry. Syracuse is named after Syracuse, Sicily, a city on the Italy island of Sicily.
