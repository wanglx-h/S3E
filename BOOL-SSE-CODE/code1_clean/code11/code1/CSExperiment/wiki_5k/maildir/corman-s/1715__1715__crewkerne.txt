'''Crewkerne''' is a town in Somerset, England where about 6,700 people live. It is in the South Somerset District, near the border with Dorset. == History == The first time it is mentioned in history is 899, when king Alfred the Great left the manor to his son :en:Ã†thelweard_(son_of_Alfred). The name ''Crewkerne'' comes from the Saxon words ''Cruce'' (cross) and ''earne'' (cottage), because of the early Church (building) there. There was also a Mint (money) where coins were made between 978 and 1035. In 1066, the manor belonged to Edith, mistress of Harold Godwinson. After the Norman Conquest, it belonged directly to William the Conqueror. == Notable Sites == :en:St_Bartholomew's_Church,_Crewkerne is located on the high ground west of the town.
