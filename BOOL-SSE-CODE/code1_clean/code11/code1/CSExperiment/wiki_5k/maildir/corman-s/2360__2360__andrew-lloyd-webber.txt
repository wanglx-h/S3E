File:2008-11-15 Эндрю Ллойд Уэббер.jpeg, November 2008]] '''Andrew Lloyd Webber, Baron Lloyd-Webber''' (born 22 March 1948) is a United Kingdom composer and impresario of Musical theater.[http://www.andrewlloydwebber.com/ The Official Andrew Lloyd Webber Web Site] His company, the Really Useful Group, is one of the largest theatre operators in London.[http://www.reallyuseful.com/ Really Useful Group] == Career == In 2018, Webber became one of fifteen people to win an Academy Award, Grammy Award, Tony Award and Emmy Award. During his career, he has produced 15 musicals (several have run at the West End theatre and on Broadway for ten or more years). His lyricists have included: Tim Rice, Don Black, Christopher Hampton, Richard Stilgoe, and Ben Elton. He wrote the music for two movies: ''Gumshoe'' and ''The Odessa File''. He wrote a Latin language requiem mass. He has won many awards for his work, most notably seven Tony Awards, three Grammy Awards, an Oscar, an International Emmy, six Olivier Awards, and a Golden Globe Award. Lloyd Webber was knighted in 1992, and entered the House of Lords in 1997. == Personal life == Lloyd Webber's second marriage was to the actor and singer Sarah Brightman. They divorced in 1990. Lloyd Webber's third and current wife is Madeleine Gurdon, a former three-day event horsewoman. His younger brother is Cello Julian Lloyd Webber. On 25 October 2009, it was reported that Webber had been diagnosis with the early stages of prostate cancer.[http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/entertainment/8324689.stm Lloyd Webber treated for cancer] He had an prostatectomy and has recovered. ==Musicals== He has many musical plays, including: * ''Joseph and the Amazing Technicolor Dreamcoat'' (1967) * ''Jesus Christ Superstar'' (1970) * ''Evita'' (1976) * ''Cats (musical)'' (1981) * ''Song and Dance'' (1982) * ''Starlight Express'' (1984) * ''Phantom of the Opera'' (1984): as an idea, (1985): Started development and 1st debut, (1986): as 2 pop singles early that year and in October 9th 1st public debut * ''Aspects of Love'' (1989) * ''Sunset Blvd.'' (1993) * ''By Jeeves'' (1996), a rewritten 1974 show * ''Whistle Down the Wind'' (1998) * ''The Beautiful Game'' (2000) * ''The Woman in White'' (2004) * ''Love Never Dies'' (2010) == References == Category:1948 births Category:Living people Category:Academy Award winning composers Category:Alumni of the University of Oxford Category:British Emmy Award winners Category:English theatre directors Category:English composers Category:Golden Globe Award winning composers Category:Theatre producers Category:British Grammy Award winners Category:Conservative Life Peers Category:People educated at Westminster School, London Category:Television personalities from London Category:Tony Award winners Category:Best Original Song Academy Award-winning songwriters
