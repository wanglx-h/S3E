Subject: Governor
A '''governor''' is a leader of some kind. In some countries which are Federation, the governor is the leader of a state or a province. For example, in the United States, all U.S. state have a governor elected by its own people. While the President of the United States is the leader of everyone in the United States, Governors are the leaders of the people living in or visiting their state. The governor is the Executive (government) for the state's administration processes. In some other countries, a governor is the title of the viceroy. This is the case in Australia, Canada and other Commonwealth realm, where the governor represents the monarch in ceremonies. In many countries such as Pakistan a provincial governor is appointed by the central government acting as the ceremonial head of the provinces. British Overseas Territories still under United Kingdom rule all have a governor who represents the monarch in that area. Category:Government occupations
