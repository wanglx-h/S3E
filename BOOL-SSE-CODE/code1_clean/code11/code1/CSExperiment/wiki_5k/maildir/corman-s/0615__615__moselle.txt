| subdivision_type1 = Country | subdivision_name1 = France, Germany, Luxembourg | length = | source1_elevation = | discharge1_avg = | basin_size = | progression = }} The '''Moselle River''' (German language: ''Mosel'') is a river which flows through France and Luxembourg, then becomes part of the Rhine River after it flows into Germany. It is long. Important cities at the Moselle river are Metz, Thionville, Trier, Bernkastel-Kues and Koblenz. The valley of the Moselle river is famous for the wine of Elbling, Riesling, and Müller-Thurgau - grapes in the wine growing region Moselle-Saar-Ruwer. == Tributaries == From the left: Madon, Terrouin, Esch (Moselle), Rupt de Mad, Orne (Moselle), Fensch (Moselle), Gander (Moselle), Syre, Sauer, Kyll, Salm River (Germany), Lieser River, Alf River, Endert, Brohlbach (Moselle), Elzbach.
