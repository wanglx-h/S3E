| birth_place = Barmen, Province of Jülich-Cleves-Berg, Kingdom of Prussia(now Wuppertal, Germany) | death_date = | death_place = London, England | party = | module = }} }} '''Friedrich Engels II''' (28 November 1820 – 5 August 1895) was a Germans Political science thinker and writer. He wrote about Communism with Philosophy Karl Marx. They wrote the book ''The Communist Manifesto'' together. == Biography == Friedrich Engels II was born in Barmen, Germany in 1820. His father was a very rich factory owner. His father sent Friedrich to England to run one of his factories. Engles became upset with the way the workers were living at the factory. He was upset with the Class struggle. At about this time, Engles began to write about politics and the struggles of the workers. In 1844, he met Karl Marx in France. The two men became friends. They also began to write together. The two men worked together until the death of Marx in 1883. Engels' influential work, "The Condition of the Working Class in England," highlighted the harsh realities of industrialization. His partnership with Marx laid the foundation for modern communism, and his writings continue to shape discussions on class struggle and societal transformation.
