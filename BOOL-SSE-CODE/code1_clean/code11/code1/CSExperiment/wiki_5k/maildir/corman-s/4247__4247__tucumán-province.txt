It is in the northwest of the country and its Capital city is San Miguel de Tucumán, often shortened to Tucumán. The province has the nickname '''El Jardín de la República''' (''The Garden of the Republic''), as it is a highly productive Farming area. ==Geography== The province of Tucumán has an area of and is surrounded by the provinces: * Salta Province to the north; * Santiago del Estero Province to the east; and * Catamarca Province to the south and west. Tucumán has two main different geographical regions. The east is associated with the Gran Chaco flat lands, while the west presents a mixture of the Sierras of the Pampas to the south and the canyons of the Argentine Northwest to the north. The highest peak in the province is the ''Cerro del Bolsón'' near the border with the Catamarca province. It has an elevation of .
