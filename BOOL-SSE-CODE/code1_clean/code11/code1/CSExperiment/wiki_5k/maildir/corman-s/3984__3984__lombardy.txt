It is the most populated region of the country, and has almost 9.4 million people. The capital is Milan. Lombardy has provinces within it. To the north of Lombardy is the country of Switzerland (Canton Ticino and Grigioni). To the west is the region of Piedmont. To the east are the regions of Veneto and Trentino-Alto Adige/Südtirol. To the south is the region of Emilia-Romagna. == Name == In the Italian language, Lombardy is called ''Lombardia''. This name comes from an old German word: ''Langbardland'' and means "Country of the Lombards". == Geography == In the north of Lombardy there are very high mountains, (40% of the territory is mountains) the highest mountains are almost 4.000 meters in the Bernina Range. In the south of the region flows the Po river, which is the longest river in Italy. The most important rivers are the Adda river, the Mincio and the Ticino (river), all of them end in the Po river. There are also a lot of lakes, the largest are the lake Maggiore, the Lake of Varese, the lake Como, and the lake Garda. == Provinces == Lombardy is divided into 12 provinces: # Province of Bergamo # Province of Brescia # Province of Como # Province of Cremona # Province of Lecco # Province of Lodi # Province of Mantua # Province of Milan # Province of Monza e Brianza # Province of Pavia # Province of Sondrio # Province of Varese ==References== == Other websites == * [http://www.regione.lombardia.it/ Official Site (IT)] Category:Lombardy
