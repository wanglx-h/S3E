Subject: Simplicity
When something is '''simple''', it is very easy to understand or to do. The opposite is ''wikt:hard'' or ''wikt:complex'' or ''wikt:difficult''. '''Simplicity''' is the quality of being simple. ''Simple'' can mean something that is only made up of a few parts. It lacks complexity. * A bicycle is much simpler than a spacecraft. ''Simple'' can mean that something or someone is humble (lowly) or common: *He lives very simply. "Simple living" is a way of life. It uses the ideas of Henry David Thoreau or Epicurus or Diogenes of Sinope as its moral wikt:goal. Simple living says that a person needs only few and basic things to be happiness. ''Simple'' can also be used to refer to someone whose brain thinks in a very simple way.
