XL Recordings, Italy Records | URL = [http://whitestripes.com www.whitestripes.com] | Current_members = Jack WhiteMeg White }} '''The White Stripes''' were an United States rock music duo from Detroit, Michigan. There were two wikt:people in the band: Jack White (Singer-songwriter, Singer, guitar, piano, and Bass guitar) and Meg White (Drum, Percussion instrument, and vocals). They are influential in the 2000s indie rock and Garage rock. The White Stripes started in Detroit, and released two albums: their The White Stripes (album) (1999) and De Stijl (album) (2000). They became famous with their third album, White Blood Cells (album) (2001). Their fourth album, Elephant (album) (2003), won the Grammy Award for Best Alternative Music Album. Their last two albums, ''Get Behind Me Satan'' (2005) and ''Icky Thump'' (2007), each won the same award. After a break, they ended in 2011. The White Stripes were known for presenting in strictly red, white, and black colors, and their frequent use of the number three. Jack and Meg White were married when they started the band, but divorced after its formation.
