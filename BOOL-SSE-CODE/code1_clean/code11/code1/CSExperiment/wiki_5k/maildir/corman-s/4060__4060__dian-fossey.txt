She is best known for studying Gorilla in Rwanda. Fossey was inspired by a book called ''The Mountain Gorilla'' by George Schaller. Fossey studied mountain gorillas in Rwanda because of the book. When people want to get close to gorillas and get to know them they have to gain the gorillas trust. Fossey changed her lifestyle to match the gorillas’. She wanted to protect the gorillas from hunters. Hunters were trespassing. She also wanted to protect the Endangered species animals in the area, even though some people didn’t agree with her ideas. She is a hero to many people for what she did to help mountain gorillas. There is a movie that was made based on what she experienced in Africa. The movie is called ''Gorillas in the Mist''. Her book, also named ''Gorillas in the Mist'', is the best-selling book on gorillas of all time. She is recognized for saving mountain gorillas and starting a successful fund to help raise money for gorillas everywhere. == Early life == Fossey was born in San Francisco. She was an only child. Her head was cut by a Machete, a weapon used by poachers. Fossey was buried at the site she created as a graveyard for the gorillas. == Legacy == Fossey studied the lives of mountain gorillas for 18 years. She was the world’s expert on mountain gorillas. Her dedication allowed for mountain gorillas to continue as a species. Some of the people who lived in the area did not like her because of her thoughts on how the gorillas should be treated.
