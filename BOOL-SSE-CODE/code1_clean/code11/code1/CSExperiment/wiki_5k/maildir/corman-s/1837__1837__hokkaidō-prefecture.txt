Sapporo is the Capital (city) of Hokkaido. ==History== Until the Meiji Restoration, the island was called Ezo, Watari-shima, Yeddo, and Yezo. Since 1869, it has been called Hokkaidō. The population expanded in the late 19th century. ===Timeline=== * '''1947''' (''Shōwa period 22''): Hokkaidō Prefecture was established.Travel-around-Japan.com, [http://www.travel-around-japan.com/k11-01-hokkaido-government.html "Former Hokkaido Government office building"]; retrieved 2012-2-14. * '''2008''' (''Heisei period 20''): The 34th G8 summit was held in Toyako, Hokkaido.Japan, Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MOFA): [http://www.mofa.go.jp/policy/economy/summit/2008/index.html 34th G8 summit, overview.] ==Geography== Hokkaidō is the 2nd largest island of Japan The island is between the Sea of Japan to the west, the Sea of Okhotsk to the northeast, the Pacific Ocean to the southeast, and the Tsugaru Strait to the south. Hokkaidō is separated from Honshu by the Tsugaru Strait; however, it is connected by the underwater Seikan tunnel. Some of the Chishima Islands are occupied by Russia, but Japan still considers the islands to be Japanese. ===National Parks=== Natural Parks are established in about 10% of the total land area of the prefecture.Japan Ministry of the Environment, [http://www.env.go.jp/en/nature/nps/park/doc/files/np_6.pdf "General overview of area figures for Natural Parks by prefecture"]; retrieved 2012-3-14. ==Shrines and Temples== ''Hokkaidō jingū'' was the chief Shinto shrine (''ichinomiya'') of Ezo, which was an ancient name for the Hokkaidō region.Richard Ponsonby-Fane.
