Huddersfield was the birthplace for the Labour Party (UK) prime minister, Harold Wilson. It is also known for its football team, who were the first team to win the Football League first division title three years in a row (1924-26). Huddersfield had 162,949 residents at the 2011 census. It sits close to the Pennines, south-west of Leeds, west of Wakefield, north-west of Sheffield and north-east of Manchester. The River Holme discharges into the River Colne, West Yorkshire.
