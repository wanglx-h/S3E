File:Flag_of_Northumberland.svg of Northumberland]] File:Arms of Northumberland County Council.svg of Northumberland]] File:Northumberland (ceremonial county) in England.svg '''Northumberland''' is the most northern county in England. Lindisfarne is an island close offshore. The county town is Morpeth. Its finest church is Hexham Abbey. Northumberland occupies a small part of the former Anglo-Saxon kingdom of Northumbria. == History == Northumberland is where Roman Britain occupiers once guarded a frontier at Hadrian's Wall. Anglian invaders fought with Celtic natives. Normans lords built Castle to suppress rebellion and defend a contested border with Scotland. The present-day county was once part of an independent Northern English kingdom that stretched from Edinburgh to the River Humber. Northumberland has more castles than any other county.Long B. 1967. ''Castles of Northumberland''. Newcastle: Harold Hill. It has greatest number of recognized battle sites. It was an Economics important region that supplied much of the coal that powered the industrial revolution. Northumberland is now a Rural area county with a small and gradually shrinking population.Dowson J. 2009. Northumberland's economy 2009. The kingdom and county were named for the River Humber. The county is noted for its undeveloped landscape of high moorland. That is a favourite with landscape painters. Now it is largely protected as a National park. Northumberland is the most sparsely populated county in England, with only 62 people per square kilometre.
