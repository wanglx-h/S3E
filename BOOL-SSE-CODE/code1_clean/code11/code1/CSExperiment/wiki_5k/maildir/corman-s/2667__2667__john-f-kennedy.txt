Miller Center|url=https://millercenter.org/president/kennedy|access-date=2023-11-07|website=millercenter.org|language=en}} ==Early life== Kennedy was born in Brookline, Massachusetts on May 29, 1917. He was the second of nine children of Kennedy Family (1888–1969). His father was a businessman and later US ambassador in the United Kingdom from 1938 until 1940. His mother was Rose Fitzgerald Kennedy (1890–1995). Kennedy graduated from Harvard University with a Bachelor's degree in International relations. Before World War II began, he tried joining the United States Army, but was rejected because he had back problems; he instead joined the United States Navy. When his PT boat was sunk by a Imperial Japanese Navy warship in 1943, he seriously injured his back. He still saved his surviving crew, and was later awarded a medal for his bravery. He was elected to the US Congress in 1946, and the US Senate in 1952. He married Jacqueline Kennedy on September 12, 1953. The couple had four children; a Stillbirth daughter (b. 1956), Caroline Bouvier Kennedy (b. 1957), John F. Kennedy Jr. (1960–1999) and Patrick, who was Premature birth in August 1963 and lived only for two days. == Presidency == Kennedy was a member of the United States Democratic Party. He beat his Republican Party (United States) opponent, Richard Nixon, in the United States presidential election, 1960. Kennedy was the youngest president ever elected. He was also the first Roman Catholic President and the first president to win a Pulitzer Prize. Kennedy was a very good speaker and inspired a new generation of young Americans, and is the only president to have been awarded a Purple Heart. === Cuban Missile Crisis === In the beginning of his term, he approved the CIA plan to Invasion Cuba. After the invasion turned out to be a failure, the Cuban Missile Crisis began. During the crisis, Cuba ordered a lot of nuclear missiles from the Soviet Union. It was the closest the world was to having a nuclear war. Kennedy ordered US Navy ships to surround Cuba. He ended the crisis peacefully by making an agreement with the Soviet Union. They agreed that the Soviet Union would stop selling nuclear weapons to Cuba. In return, the U.S. would take its missiles out of Turkey and promise to never invade Cuba again. === Other actions === Kennedy also created a plan called the New Frontier. This was a series of government programs, such as urban renewal, to help Poverty and working class people. He created the Peace Corps to help poor countries all over the world. He agreed to a large tax cut to help the economy. He also called for the Civil Rights Act of 1964, which would make discrimination and segregation illegal. Kennedy intended to reach a detente with Cuban Premier, Fidel Castro, and to withdraw all US military advisers from Vietnam.[http://consortiumnews.com/2013/11/25/jfks-embrace-of-third-world-nationalists/ JFK's embrace of third world nationalists] == Assassination == Kennedy was assassinated on November 22, 1963, in Dallas, Texas. He was being driven through the city in an open-top car, along with John Connally, the Governor of Texas. As the car drove into Dealey Plaza, Gunshot were fired. Kennedy was shot once in the throat and once in the head. He was taken to Parkland Memorial Hospital away. At 1:00 p.m., Kennedy was pronounced dead. Lee Harvey Oswald, a former United States Marine Corps, was the prime suspect in the murder, and he was arrested on the same day for the murder of a policeman called J. D. Tippit. Oswald denied shooting anyone and was killed two days later on November 24 by Jack Ruby. Kennedy had a state funeral on November 25, three days after his murder, near to the White House. He was buried in Arlington National Cemetery in Arlington, Virginia. == Legacy == After Kennedy died, Lyndon Johnson (his Vice President of the United States) took over and put many of Kennedy's ideas into law (see Great Society). Kennedy was a very popular president and still is today.
