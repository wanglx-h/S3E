It is home to over 1.6 million people. It was known as ''Salisbury'' from its foundation on September 12, 1890 until April 18, 1982. Harare is Zimbabwe's biggest and most important financial, commercial, and communications centre, and a trade centre for tobacco, maize, cotton, and citrus fruits. Manufactured goods include textiles, steel and chemicals, and gold is mined in the area. The city's suburbs include Borrowdale, Mount Pleasant and Avondale. The richest neighbourhoods are to the north. The University of Zimbabwe, the country's oldest university (founded in 1952), is in Mount Pleasant, about 6km (3.7mi) north of the city centre. The country's main Test cricket ground, Harare Sports Club is in the city.
