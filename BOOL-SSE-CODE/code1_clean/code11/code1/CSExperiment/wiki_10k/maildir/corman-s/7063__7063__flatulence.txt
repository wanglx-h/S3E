Subject: Flatulence
File:Human fart.wav '''Flatulence''' (commonly known as '''Farting''' or '''passing gas''') is when gas leaves the anus. This sometimes happens with a sound that can be wikt:embarrassing to some people, but in some cultures it is treated like humor. The word flatulence comes from the Latin word ''flatus'', which means wikt:blow or wikt:breath, but people today usually call it wikt:fart. Children normally pass gas ''30'' ''times'' a day as their digestive system is not fully developed. Adults on the other hand only let wind approximately 15 times a day. In mammals like humans, the gases come from two sources: swallowed air (for example, while eating, talking or drinking) and gases naturally created by bacteria in the body during digestion.
