Re-erected on the A38(M) in Birmingham, England]] '''Steam''' is the name given to water when it is in a gas form. Steam is sometimes thought of as a cloud of translucent mist, however that is only the result of the steam Condensation in the air, forming water vapor. Actual hot steam is invisible. ==Introduction== When the pressure of the atmosphere is 1013 mBar (unit) – average pressure for a place which is at sea level – water will Boiling to become steam at 100 Celsius. This is the boiling point. Boiling happens in a boiler. 100 Celsius is the same temperature as 212 Fahrenheit, 80 Réaumur and 373.15 Kelvin. Steam is mainly used for Power (physics) up Steam engine. A steam engine is a heat engine performing Work (physics) using steam as its working fluid. ==Heating food== Steam gets sometimes used for heating food. In a pressure cooker, some water turns into steam, and some steam gets released (or let out) thru a valve.
