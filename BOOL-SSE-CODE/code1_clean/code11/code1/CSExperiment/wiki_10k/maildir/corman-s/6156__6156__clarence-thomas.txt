Regardless, Thomas was confirmed in October 1991. == Early life == Clarence Thomas was born in Pin Point, Georgia, Georgia (U.S. state). His father left his family when he was only two years old.Hall, Timothy L., ''Supreme Court Justices: A Biographical Dictionary'', Facts on File, Inc., New York, New York, page 421 He did not meet his son Clarence again until Clarence was nine years old.Thomas, Clarence, ''My Grandfather's Son: A Memoir'', HarperCollins Publishers New York, New York, 2007, page 1 This left his mother, Leola Anderson, to take care of the family. Clarence and his brother were sent to live with his mother's parents in Savannah, Georgia. His grandfather wanted him to become a priest. Thomas was sent to St. John Vianney Minor Seminary, an all-Caucasian boarding school near Savannah. In 1968 he transferred to the College of the Holy Cross in Worcester, Massachusetts. He gave up all ideas of becoming a priest after the death of Martin Luther King Jr.. Thomas then obtained a law degree at Yale University. == Career == After Yale he returned to Missouri and worked for State Attorney General, John Danforth. in 1977 Danforth was elected to the United States Senate. Thomas became a corporate lawyer for the Monsanto Company. Two years later he became Danforth's legislative aide in Washington, D.C.. President of the United States Ronald Reagan offered Thomas a job as the assistant secretary for civil rights in the United States Department of Education. He then became the chairman of the U.S. Equal Employment Opportunity Commission. Thomas was appointed to the United States Court of Appeals for the District of Columbia Circuit in 1990 by President George H. W. Bush. In 1991, Thurgood Marshall retirement from the Supreme Court.
