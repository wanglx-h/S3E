File:Japanese school uniform dsc06051.jpg '''Uniforms''' are special clothes to show that a group of people wikt:belong together. The group of people will all be dressed in the same way, where ''uni-'' means "one", and ''form'' means "shape". ==Introduction== People may wear uniforms for several reasons. The uniform will help the people to feel a team wikt:spirit so that they work well together. They may also help members of the :wikt:public to know who they are, e.g. in a department store a wikt:customer can tell who is a member of Employee, or in the street people will recognize a Police. Uniforms may also be worn because they are wikt:practical, e.g. it might keep them safe when operating machinery or keep them clean when doing their work. ==Examples== ===General=== Soldier wear uniforms. They also wear things such as Badge and Insignia on their uniform to show their wikt:rank, wikt:specialty, military unit, and other information about them. Nurse in Hospital wear uniforms. Domestic worker sometimes wear uniforms when working for their employer. Important domestic workers may wear special smart uniforms called "livery" e.g. porters (doormen) at wikt:luxury Hotel. ===By region=== In some countries, such as India, Japan, China, Korea, Australia and the United Kingdom, many school children wear uniforms. These uniforms tend to be a set of Clothing with the school crest or symbol. It helps them feel wikt:proud of their school and children from wikt:rich families and poor families all look the same. In Britain, for example, most young children wear school uniforms. In State school. the children may wear polo-necked T-shirt with a school logo. Other schools, especially Private school, may have a wikt:formal uniform, or they may be free to choose their own clothes so long as they are the school Colour. Category:Uniforms
