Subject: Andrew Aagesen
File:Andreas Aagesen 1879.jpg '''Andrew Aagesen''' or '''Andreas Aagesen''' (August 5, 1826 - October 26, 1879) was a Denmark lawyer and politician. The Danish laws of the Sea, of the 1882 was mostly his work. == Biography == Andrew Aagesen studied law at Christianshavn and Copenhagen. He stopped his studies in 1848 to take part in the First Schleswig War. He served as the leader of a wikt:reserve battalion. In 1855, he became a professor of jurisprudence at the University of Copenhagen. In 1879, he was elected as a member of the Landsting. He was Carl Christian Hall's wikt:successor as a lecturer on Roman law at the University of Copenhagen.
