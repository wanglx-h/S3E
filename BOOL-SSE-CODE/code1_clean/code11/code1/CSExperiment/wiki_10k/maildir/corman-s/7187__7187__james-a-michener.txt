Subject: James A. Michener
File:James Albert Michener · DN-SC-92-05368.JPEG '''James Albert Michener''' ( or ; February 3, 1907 – October 16, 1997) was an Americans writer. His books include ''Tales of the South Pacific'', ''Hawaii (novel)'', ''The Drifters (novel)'', ''Texas (novel)'', and ''Poland (novel)''. Most of his 40 books are very large story. They are about the lives of many generations in a particular place. His non-fiction writings include the 1992 book ''The World is My Home'' and ''Sports in America''. Michener wrote that he did not know who his parents were or exactly when and where he was born. He was raised by an adoption mother, Mabel Michener, in Doylestown, Pennsylvania, Pennsylvania. Some have argued that Mabel was his birth mother. He Graduation from Swarthmore College, where he played basketball, in 1929. He later studied at the University of Northern Colorado. He taught there for several years. He also taught at Harvard University. His writing career began during World War II. He was assigned to the Pacific Ocean as a navy historian. He used his time there as the basis for ''Tales of the South Pacific'', his first book. This book was the basis for the musical ''South Pacific (musical)''. Michener met his wife Mari while in Japan. His novel ''Sayonara'' is autobiography. On January 10, 1977, he was given the Presidential Medal of Freedom by Gerald R. Ford. In his final years, he lived in Austin, Texas, where he died of kidney failure on October 16, 1997 at the age of 90. == Books by James A.
