Subject: Conn Smythe Trophy
File:Hhof connsmythe.jpg The '''Conn Smythe Trophy''' is a trophy in the National Hockey League awarded to the most valuable player in the playoffs each year. It was first awarded in 1965. Montreal Canadiens and Colorado Avalanche goaltender Patrick Roy has won it three times, more than any other player. Normally the trophy goes to a player of the Stanley Cup champion. Sometimes it goes to someone on the losing team.
