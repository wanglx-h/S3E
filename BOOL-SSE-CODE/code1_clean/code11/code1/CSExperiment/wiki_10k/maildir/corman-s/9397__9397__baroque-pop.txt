Subject: Baroque pop
'''Baroque pop''' is a rock music subgenre that mixes both Rock music and classical music. It was created by musicians in the 1960s by adding classical instruments like harpsichord, Horns, oboes, and String Section. It became popular with both teenagers and adults at the time. Examples can be found on The Beach Boys' ''Pet Sounds'' and recordings made by Phil Spector. It is also referred to as '''chamber pop''', among other names. ==References== Category:Music genres
