Soon he moved on to writing for television series, including ''The Dick Van Dyke Show'' and ''The Tonight Show''. In 1970, he adapted the Neil Simon play and 1968 movie ''The Odd Couple'' into a popular television series starring Tony Randall and Jack Klugman. It was very successful. Marshall worked on ''Happy Days'', ''Laverne and Shirley'' (which co-starred his sister Penny Marshall), and ''Mork and Mindy''. Both of there were spin-offs from ''Happy Days'' and just as successful and well-known. He movie director and acted in several movies including ''Pretty Woman'' and ''Runaway Bride''.
