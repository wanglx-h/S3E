The year '''1 AD''' ('''Roman numeral''') was a common year starting on Saturday in the Julian calendar. The year started on a common year starting on Monday in the Gregorian calendar. It was the first year of the 1st century and 1st millennium. It is one of only seven years to use just one Roman numeral. The seven are '''1 AD''' (I), 5 (V), 10 (X), 50 (L), 100 (C), 500 (D), and 1000 (M). At the time, it was known as the ''Year of the Roman consul of Gaius Caesar and Lucius Aemilius Paullus (consul 1)''. The denomination 1 for this year has been used since the early Middle Ages, when the Anno Domini calendar era became the Method for naming years.
