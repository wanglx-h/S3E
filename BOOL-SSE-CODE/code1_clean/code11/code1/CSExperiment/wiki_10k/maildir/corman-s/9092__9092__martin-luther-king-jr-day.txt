Subject: Martin Luther King Jr. Day
File:President Ronald Reagan at the Signing Ceremony for Martin Luther King Holiday Legislation.jpg and Coretta Scott King at the Martin Luther King Jr. Day signing ceremony.]] '''Martin Luther King, Jr. Day''' is a United States Federal Holiday. It commemorates civil rights activist Martin Luther King, Jr.'s birthday. It is celebrated on the third Monday in January and is one of only four United States Federal holidays to honor an individual person.The other three federal holidays honoring individuals are George Washington Birthday (which is usually called called ''President's Day''), Columbus Day, and Christmas. Most people working in banks and other government owned places get the day off. Many students get a day off from school on this day. See [http://www4.law.cornell.edu/uscode/html/uscode05/usc_sec_05_00006103----000-.html 5 USC 6103]. Ronald Reagan signed a bill, proposed by Katie Hall (politician) of Indiana, to create a federal holiday honoring King. The holiday was observed for the first time on January 20, 1986. It is observed on the third Monday of January. ==References== Category:January observances
