She has won five world championships and nine U.S. championships. She was the List of ambassadors of the United States to Belize from 2022 to 2025. == Birth and childhood == Michelle Kwan was born on July 7, 1980, in Torrance, California, California. Kwan showed interest in figure stating when she followed Ron and Karen, her brother and sister, onto the ice, saying that all she could remember was "every day, tying up my skates and a big smile on my face, excited to go on the ice". She attended public school until eighth grade, when she began being homeschooling in 1994. == Junior world champion == Kwan won the world junior championship in 1994. The same year, at the age of 13, she came in the top 10 in the world senior championship. She became world champion in 1996, when she was 15. ==Diplomatic career== On December 15, 2021, President Joe Biden announced that he planned to nominate Kwan to be List of ambassadors of the United States to Belize. == Olympics == * World Championships: first place in 1996, 1998, 2000, 2001, 2003 * Olympic Games: 1998 - silver; 2002 - bronze * U.S. Championships: first place in 1996, 1998, 1999, 2000, 2001, 2002, 2003, 2004, 2005 == References == == Other websites == * * *[http://www.usfigureskating.org/AthleteBio.asp?id=2267 United States Figure Skating Association (USFSA) Athlete Biography] *[http://www.isufs.org/bios/isufs00000561.htm International Skating Union (ISU) Skater Biography] *[http://www.usoc.org/26_1145.htm US Olympic Committee Biography] *[http://www.MichelleKwan.info/ Michelle Kwan Fan Site (1)] *[http://heatherw.com/mk/ Michelle Kwan Fan Site (2)] Category:Figure skaters from California Category:Olympic bronze medalists for the United States Category:Olympic silver medalists for the United States Category:Sportspeople from Los Angeles Category:Politicians from Los Angeles Category:Ambassadors of the United States Category:1980 births Category:Living people
