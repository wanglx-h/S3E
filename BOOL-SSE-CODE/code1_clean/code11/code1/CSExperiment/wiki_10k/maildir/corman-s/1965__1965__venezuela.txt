The official language is Spanish language, and the capital city is Caracas. Other languages spoken in Venezuela include Carib, Guahibo, Warao, Wayuu, Pemon, and Piaroa. == Geography == The country has a wide range of geography that includes Island in the Caribbean Sea, Coast areas, Highland, and parts of the Andes. Venezuela is famous for being the home of Angel Falls, the world's tallest waterfall, in the Bolívar (state). == Economy == Venezuela's economy is based on oil, cotton, cocoa, sugar, coffee, and tobacco. Venezuela has one of the largest oil supplies in the world, and it produces a large amount of oil. It also has a very large supply of Fossil fuel, gasoline, and Mineral. == People and history == Ancient history Venezuelans came from the east, west, south, and north. Christopher Columbus was the first European to enter Venezuela, but there had been Indigenous peoples living there for a long time. === European colonialism === ''Also see: Colonialism and Atlantic slave trade'' When white Europeans (like the British Empire, Spanish Empire, and French colonial empire) conquered territory in Americas, they took their black Africa Slavery to America to work. The Spanish Empire conquered Venezuela and Atlantic slave trade from Africa. At this time, black people had no rights; they just worked for food. Because of the Colonialism period, Venezuela's population today is very Race (sociology) mixed. The Spanish taught Catholic Church, and many native people Religious conversion. Today, almost all Venezuelans (96%) are Catholics; just 2% are Protestantism. For a long time, society was headed by white Creole peoples: people in Venezuela who were descended from Spaniards, but considered Venezuela home. Other Venezuelans did not like that, and they started thinking about independence. On July 24, 1823 Venezuela won its independence, led by Simón Bolívar. === Modern history === Venezuela's modern history was shaped by a number of people, including President of Venezuela Rómulo Betancourt, who replaced a military dictatorship with a democracy. By the 1970s, Venezuela had become rich from oil revenue, but it had problems in the 1980s and 1990s. In 1999 Hugo Chávez became president and tried to remake Venezuela into a socialist state. While popular at first, the economic changes made by Chavez and the next president, Maduro, caused a massive Depression (economics) in the mid-2010s.
