Constantinople was located in the Bosporus, the strait between the Balkans and Asia Minor. == History == For centuries the city was not very large, and was called Byzantium. In the 4th century, List of Roman emperors Constantine the Great made Byzantium the capital of the Roman Empire and renamed it to '''Constantinople'''. In the 13th century, Constantinople was Sack of Constantinople by Crusades during the Fourth Crusade. The crusaders created the Latin Empire (12041261), with Constantinople as the capital city. In 1261, the Empire of Nicaea successfully restored the Byzantine Empire, under the rule of the Palaiologos. In 1453 the Ottoman sultan Mehmed II Fall of Constantinople Constantinople. The city was renamed Istanbul and became the capital of the Ottoman Empire (14531922). Over time, the city's short name in became the name Istanbul.
