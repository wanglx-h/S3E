File:Hubble - infant galaxy.jpg '''Cosmology''' is the branch of astronomy that deals with the universe. NASA defines cosmology as "The study of the structure and changes in the present universe". Another definition of cosmology is "the study of the universe, and humanity's place in it". Modern cosmology is dominated by the Big Bang theory, which brings together observational astronomy and particle physics. Though the word ''cosmology'' is recent (first used in 1730 in Christian Wolff's ''Cosmologia Generalis''), the study of the universe has a long history. ==History== Until the Renaissance people thought the universe was only the planets up to Saturn (planet), and stars. With the invention of the telescope, we could see more of the universe. Early in the 20th century, astronomers thought the Milky Way was the entire universe. Later, with astrophotography and spectroscopy, astronomers (for example Edwin Hubble) showed that the Milky Way was only one of many galaxies. Modern cosmology is considered to have started in 1915 with the final paper of Albert Einstein General relativity. This made physicists realize that the universe changed. When a scientific discipline begins to change an idea that is believed by many people, it is known as a Paradigm Shift. Many scientists debated if there were other galaxies.
