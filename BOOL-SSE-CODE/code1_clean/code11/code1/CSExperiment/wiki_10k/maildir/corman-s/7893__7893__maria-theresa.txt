She was also queen of Hungary and Bohemia, archduchess of Austria, and held many other titles. She changed the royal palace outside Vienna (Austria's Capital city) to look much like Versailles. Vienna itself became an important center for the Art, especially classical music. Maria Theresa added support to her Absolutism by tightening her hold on the government. She also improved conditions for the Peasant. She is generally known to history as the '''Empress Maria Theresa'''. ==Biography== File:Marie-Thérèse d'Autriche (1717-1780), atelier d'Anton von Maron.jpgFile:Maria Theresa ein kreutzer 1760 obverse.jpgMaria Theresa was born in Vienna, Austria, on 13 May 1717. Her parents were Charles VI, Holy Roman Emperor and Elisabeth Christine of Brunswick-Wolfenbüttel. Her father had made the Pragmatic Sanction of 1713, a decree which allowed a female ruler despite the Salic law's prohibition. Maria Theresa married Duke Francis Stephan I of Lorraine for love.
