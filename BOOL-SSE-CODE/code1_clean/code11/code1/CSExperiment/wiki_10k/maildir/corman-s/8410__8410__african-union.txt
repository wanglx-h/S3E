Subject: African Union
File:African Union (orthographic projection).svg The '''African Union''' ('''AU''') is an supranational organization consisting of fifty-five African member countries. Founded in Durban on 9 July 2002, the AU was formed as a replacement to the :en:Organisation_of_African_Unity (OAU) and the :en:African_Economic_Community (AEC). The current Chairperson of the African Union is President of Mauritania of Mauritania Mohamed Ould Ghazouani since February 17, 2024. The AU, in the future, aims to have a Single Currency and a Single :en:Integration Defense force, as well as other Institutions of State, including a cabinet for the AU Head of State. The purpose of the organization is to help secure the continent's Democracy, Human rights, and a Sustainability Economy, especially by bringing an end to conflict between African Nations and creating an effective :en:Common_market_(disambiguation).
