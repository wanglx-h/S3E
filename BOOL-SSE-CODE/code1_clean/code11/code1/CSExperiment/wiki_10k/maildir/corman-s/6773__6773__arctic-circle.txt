Image:World map with arctic circle.svg The '''Arctic Circle''' is one of the five major circle of latitude (imaginary lines around the Earth) that are often marked on maps of the Earth. This is the parallel of latitude that (in 2000) runs degree (angle) north of the Equator. North of this line, the Sun never rises at the Winter Solstice and never sets at the Summer Solstice. The Arctic Circle is one way to say what part of the Earth is in the Arctic. The North Pole is in the center of the Arctic Circle. country which are partly within the Arctic Circle are: *Russia *Canada *Denmark (Greenland) *United States (Alaska) *Norway *Sweden *Finland *Iceland (less than 1 square kilometre) The position of the Arctic Circle is not fixed. It directly depends on the Earth's axial tilt. The axial tilt fluctuates within a margin of 2Â° over a 40,000-year period, mostly due to tidal forces from the orbit of the Moon.
