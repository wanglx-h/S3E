He was born into a Jewish family in London. He was coming out gay.[http://www.guardian.co.uk/news/2003/jul/26/guardianobituaries.filmnews Guardian obituary] He died of a stroke in Palm Springs, California, United States.[http://www.notablebiographies.com/newsmakers2/2004-Q-Z/Schlesinger-John.html Encyclopedia of World Biography] Schlesinger's acting career began in the 1950s. He started to direct in 1956. By the 1960s he was not acting so he could just direct. His documentary film ''Terminus (film)'' (1961), got a Venice Film Festival Gold Lion and a British Academy of Film and Television Arts. ''A Kind of Loving'' won the Golden Bear award at the 12th Berlin International Film Festival in 1962. Schlesinger's movie, ''Midnight Cowboy'' (1969), made in the United States, was internationally successful. A story of two hustlers living on the fringe in the bad side of New York City, it was Schlesinger's first movie shot in the U.S. It won Academy Awards for Academy Award for Directing and Academy Award for Best Picture.
