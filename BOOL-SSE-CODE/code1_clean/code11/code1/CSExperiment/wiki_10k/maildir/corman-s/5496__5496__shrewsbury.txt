It is located 9 miles (14km) east of the Wales border and serves as the commercial centre for Shropshire and mid-Wales. The town is very old. It was started around 700. The town centre has almost the exact same medieval street plan and over 660 listed buildings. Many of these buildings have timber framing from the 15th and 16th centuries. There is also Shrewsbury Castle, which was built in 1074, and Shrewsbury Abbey, which was built in 1083. The town centre is almost completely surrounded by the River Severn. Charles Darwin was born in Shrewsbury and lived there for 27 years. The population of Shrewsbury is just over 76,000, making it the second largest town in Shropshire, the biggest being Telford. The town has a Shrewsbury railway station and a professional Association football club, Shrewsbury Town F.C., who compete in Football League One. Shrewsbury is pronounced differently between different people - including the locals - many pronounce the town the town as ‘Shroosbri’ whereas other will pronounce it as ‘Shrowsbri’. ==Twinning== Shrewsbury is Twin town with Zutphen, Netherlands. ==In fiction== Shrewsbury Abbey is the setting of the Brother Cadfael Chronicles by Ellis Peters (Edith Pargeter). The books were later made into a television show. The story is about a 12th-century monk who solves mysteries.
