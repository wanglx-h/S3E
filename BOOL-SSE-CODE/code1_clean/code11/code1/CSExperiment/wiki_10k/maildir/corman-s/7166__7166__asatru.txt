File:Sigurblót 2009.JPG '''Asatru''' (Icelandic: '''Ásatrú''', Norwegian: '''Åsatru''', Swedish: '''Asatro''') is a religion which involves the worship of wikt:ancient Germanic and Scandinavia Vikings spirits and Æsir. It received official legal establishment in 1970s in Iceland through the efforts of :en:Sveinbjörn_Beinteinsson and other Icelanders. The current recognized national organization of Asatru in Iceland is Ásatrúarfélagið. The name is derived from two Old Norse words. It means "faith in the Æsir," the Old Norse deity.https://icelandmag.is/article/11-things-know-about-present-day-practice-asatru-ancient-religion-vikings A person who practices Asatru is called an ''Asatruar'', and is sometimes referred to as a Heathen. Asatru is also called Wodanism, Wotanism or Germanic paganism.https://viking-styles.com/blogs/history/what-is-asatru Common books that describe the knowledge in depth include the Sagas and the Eddas. == Gods and Goddesses == Asatru is a Polytheism religion in which various goddesses and gods are worshiped. The most prominent are Thor, Odin, Freya, Frigg, Freyr, Tyr, Loki, and Heimdall. ==References== Category:Paganism
