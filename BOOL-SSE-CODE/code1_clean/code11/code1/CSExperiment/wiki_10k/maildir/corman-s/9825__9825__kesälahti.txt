Subject: Kesälahti
Image:Kesälahti.vaakuna.svg '''Kesälahti''' is a former municipality in Finland. It is part of the North Karelia region. There are almost 2,700 people living in Kesalahti. == History == Kesalahti is the southernmost municipality of North Karelia. It was officially founded in 1873. The area of the municipality is 582.67km². 195.42km² of it is water. Kesälahti is advertised as a popular summer destination for families enjoying camping. The Puruvesi lake shores host three active camping grounds with cabins,¨and tent and trailer lots to let. The name of the municipality can be translated as 'summer bay'. The current mayor is Jorma Turunen.
