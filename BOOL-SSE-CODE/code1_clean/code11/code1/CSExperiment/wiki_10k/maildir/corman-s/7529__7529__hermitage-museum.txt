|established = |location = 34 Palace Embankment, Dvortsovy Municipal Okrug, Tsentralny District, Saint Petersburg, Saint Petersburg, Russia |visitors = 1,649,443 visitors (2021)The Art Newspaper annual survey, March 28, 2022. |director = Mikhail Piotrovsky |publictransit = Admiralteyskaya (Saint Petersburg Metro) |website = }} {| align="right" | File:Stp winter palace.jpg overlooks the Neva River.]] |- | |- | File:Hermitage interior.jpg |} The '''Hermitage Museum''' (Эрмитаж) in Saint Petersburg, Russia is one of the largest and oldest art gallery and museums of human history and culture in the world. The vast Hermitage collections are displayed in six buildings, founded by Catherine II of Russia in 1764 and opened in public since 1852. The main building is the Winter Palace. This was the official residence of the Russian Tsars. == Collections == Strong points of the Hermitage collection of Western art include Michelangelo Buonarroti, Giambattista Pittoni, Leonardo da Vinci, Peter Paul Rubens, Anthony van Dyck, Rembrandt Harmenszoon van Rijn, Nicolas Poussin, Claude Lorraine, Antoine Watteau, Giovanni Battista Tiepolo, Canaletto, Antonio Canova, Auguste Rodin, Claude Monet, Camille Pissarro, Pierre-Auguste Renoir, Paul Cézanne, Vincent van Gogh, Paul Gauguin, Pablo Picasso, and Henri Matisse. There are several more collections. Among the collections are the Russian imperial regalia, an assortment of Peter Carl Fabergé jewellery, and the largest existing collection of ancient gold from Eastern Europe and Western Asia. == Cats == Some cats lives on the museum grounds. They are named the Hermitage cats. They are an added attraction for tourists.
