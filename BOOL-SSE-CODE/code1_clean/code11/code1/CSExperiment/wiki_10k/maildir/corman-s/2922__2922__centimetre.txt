Subject: Centimetre
A '''centimetre''' (the United States spelling is '''centimeter''', symbol '''cm''') is a unit of length in the International System of Units (SI) measurement system. It is equal to one hundredth of a metre. Centi- is placed in front to say 'a hundredth'. The centimeter is the base unit of length in the old Centimetre-gram-second system of units (CGS) system of units. The centimetre is usually used to measure things that are too big for millimetre but too small for metres. A centimetre is close to the length of a fingernail. ==Similarities to other units of length== Image:CarpentersRule.png 1 centimetre is ''equal'' to: * 10 millimetres * 0.01 metre (1 metre is equal to 100 centimetres) * about 0.393700787401575 inch (1 inch is equal to 2.54 centimetres) [http://scienceworld.wolfram.com/physics/Inch.html Inch - from Eric Weisstein's World of Physics] 1 cubic centimetre is equal to 1 litre, under the SI system of units. ==Uses of centimetre== The centimetre is also used: * sometimes, to measure how much rain has fallen into a rain gauge;"[http://www.weathershack.com/education/rain-measurement.html Rain Measurement, Rain Gauge, Wireless Rain Gauge, Rain Gage, Rain Gauge Data] ".
