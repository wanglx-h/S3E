Subject: Duodenum
File:Tractus intestinalis duodenum.svg The '''duodenum''' is a short section of the small intestine, and is where most digestion in the small intestine takes place. It receives Secretion, including bile and juice, from the pancreas and the liver through the pancreatic and common bile Duct. These secretions limit, or Base (chemistry), the stomach Acid. Also, enzymes from the liver help to digest the food. Category:Anatomy of the digestive system
