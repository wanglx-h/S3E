|image_plan = Konstanz in KN.svg |state = Baden-Württemberg |region = Freiburg |district = Konstanz |elevation = 405 |area = 55.65 |population = 81692 |Stand = 2014-12-31 |postal_code = 78462–78467 |area_code = 07531, 07533 |licence = KN |Gemeindeschlüssel = 08 3 35 043 |divisions = 15 |website = |mayor = Ulrich Burchardt |Bürgermeistertitel = Oberbürgermeister |party = Christian Democratic Union of Germany }} '''Konstanz''' (English language often '''Constance''') is a university town with a population of around 80,000. It is on the shore of Lake Constance, in the south-west corner of Germany, near Switzerland. The history of the city goes back to Ancient Rome. In the 15th century, the Council of Constance was held there and ended the Western Schism.
