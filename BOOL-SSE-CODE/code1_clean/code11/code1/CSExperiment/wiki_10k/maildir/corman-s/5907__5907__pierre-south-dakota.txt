As of the 2020 census, it had a population of 14,091. Pierre is the county seat of Hughes County, South Dakota. Started in 1880 on the Missouri River across from Fort Pierre, Pierre has been the state capital since 1889. Pierre lies on rough river bluffs overlooking the Missouri River. It is a few miles away from Lake Oahe, one of the largest man-made lakes in the world, and a very popular fishing destination. == Transportation == Image:Pierre capitol3.jpg Image:Pierre capitol4.jpg Two airlines serving Pierre Regional Airport provide non-stop flights to Denver, Colorado and Minneapolis, Minnesota. The nearest interstate highway is Interstate 90, about 34 miles south of Pierre via U.S. Route 83. Pierre is one of only four state capitals not served by an interstate highway.The other state capitals without Interstate highways are Dover, Delaware; Jefferson City, Missouri; and Juneau, Alaska. ==Notes== ==References== == Other websites == * [http://ci.pierre.sd.us/ City of Pierre official website] Category:State capitals in the United States Category:County seats in South Dakota Category:Cities in South Dakota Category:1880 establishments in Dakota Territory
