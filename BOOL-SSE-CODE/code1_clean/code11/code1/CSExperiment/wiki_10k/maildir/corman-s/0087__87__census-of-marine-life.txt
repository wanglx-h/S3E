Subject: Census of Marine Life
The '''Census of Marine Life''' was a ten-year survey of life in the oceans, starting in 2000. Its head was Ron O'Dor of Dalhousie University in Halifax Regional Municipality, Canada. It used data from researchers all over the world. More than 70 nations were involved and over a 1,000,000,000 United States dollar were spent on it. It was a major work of marine ecology. It was founded by J. Frederick Grassle. The purpose of the Census of Marine Life was to say what is alive in our seas and oceans. == Other websites == * [http://www.coml.org/ Census of Marine Life website] Category:Biological oceanography Category:Censuses
