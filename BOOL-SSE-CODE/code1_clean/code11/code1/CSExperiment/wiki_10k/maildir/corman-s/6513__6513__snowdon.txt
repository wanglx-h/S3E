Its top is known as ''Yr Wyddfa'' (Old Welsh meaning "the grave"). The English language name Snowdon comes from Anglo-Saxon "Snow Dun", meaning "snow hill". It is in Snowdonia. As the highest mountain in Wales, Snowdon is one of three mountains climbed as part of the Three Peaks Challenge. ==References== Category:Mountains of the United Kingdom Category:Geography of Wales
