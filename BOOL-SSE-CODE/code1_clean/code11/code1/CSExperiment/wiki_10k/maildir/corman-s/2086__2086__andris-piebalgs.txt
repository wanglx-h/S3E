File:Andris Piebalgs on March 31, 2010.jpg '''Andris Piebalgs''' (born 17 September 1957, in Valmiera) is a Latvian politician. Since 22 November 2004, he has been the European Commissioner for Energy. He was born in Valmiera and was education at the University of Latvia in Riga. He worked as a teacher in Valmiera in the 1980s. After Latvia became independence from the Soviet Union he was education Minister (government) (in charge of schools) from 1990 to 1993, and finance minister (in charge of money) from 1994 to 1995. He was Latvia's ambassador to Estonia from 1995 to 1997, and to the European Union from 1998 to 2003, when he helped Latvia join the European Union. He also helped to start a political party called the Latvian Way. He speaks Latvian language, English language, German language, French language, and Russian language, and some Estonian language.
