Subject: Vietnamese Declaration of Independence
The '''Declaration of Independence of the Democratic Republic of Vietnam''' was a speech read by Ho Chi Minh on September 2, 1945, in Ba Dinh Square, Hanoi, Vietnam, which proclaims the independence of the Democratic Republic of Vietnam from France and Japan Empire after the Second World War. ==History== File:Président Ho-chi-Minh lit la Proclamation-d'indépendance sur la place Ba-dinh le 2nd Sep 1945.jpg on September 2, 1945.]] During the August Revolution, Ho Chi Minh wrote the speech at 48 Hang Ngang Street, Hanoi, at the home of a bourgeoisie family that donated 5,147 taels of gold to the Vietnamese Revolution headed by Ho Chi Minh. The Declaration was written with the advice of OSS Detachment 101 Maj. Archimedes Patti. It is based on the American Declaration of Independence and the Declaration of the Rights of Man and Citizen. ==Text== ==References== Category:1945 in Vietnam Category:Speeches Category:North Vietnam Category:Declarations of independence
