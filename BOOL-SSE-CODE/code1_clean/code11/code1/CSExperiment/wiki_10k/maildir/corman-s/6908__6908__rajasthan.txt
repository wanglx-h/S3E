It is the largest state of India in terms of land area; 132,150 square mile (342,269 km²). It is bigger than the Republic of the Congo but smaller than Germany. In traditional India geography it falls under the North Indian zone. The Thar Desert covers most parts of the state. The capital of the state is city of Jaipur. Rajasthan is known for its rich history, vibrant culture, and barren landscapes. With a population of over 68 million(2011), Rajasthan is home to a diverse group of people, including the Rajput, Jats, Meenas, and Bhil people, who have contributed to the region’s unique identity and cultural heritage.
