Time: Nine minutes]] File:Inachis io-02 (xndr).jpg A '''pupa''' (plural: '''pupae''' or '''pupas''') is part of becoming an adult for some insects, like some butterfly, moths, beetles, fly, and bees. The pupal stage comes after being a larva; the next stage is adulthood. The pupa stage of a butterfly, called a chrysalis (plural "chrysalids"), is a well-known example of a pupa because they are often found in the open.
