Subject: Christoph Willibald von Gluck
Image:Joseph Siffred Duplessis - Christoph Willibald Gluck - Google Art Project.jpg, dated 1775 (Kunsthistorisches Museum, Vienna)]] '''Christoph Willibald Ritter von Gluck''' (born in Erasbach, July 2, 1714; died in Vienna, November 15, 1787) was a Germany composer. He is famous for his operas which were written in a new and exciting way. He was not interested in writing operas which were just a series of songs for singers to show off. He wanted to make his operas into good pieces of drama. His operas had a big influence on later composers like Mozart, Carl Maria von Weber and Richard Wagner. He wrote Italian operas and French operas. Category:1714 births Category:1787 deaths Category:18th-century German composers Category:Classical era composers
