Subject: Poet
A '''poet''' is someone who writes poetry. Poetry can be written in any language by a poet. Sappho was an Ancient Greece Greek poet. She lived in Lesbos, Greece. She was famous for writing Lyric poetry love poems to women. She wrote in the Ancient Greek language. Famous English language poets are William Shakespeare, Alexander Pope, William Wordsworth, William Blake, John Keats, and Percy Bysshe Shelley. They wrote in the English language. Their poetry comes in different styles and forms. Often their poetry is Lyric poetry and Rhyme. The most famous American poetry include Emily Dickinson and Walt Whitman. They wrote in English. Often their poetry did not rhyme. They lived in the 19th century in the United States. Some 20th century American poets include Ezra Pound, T. S. Eliot, Sylvia Plath, Anne Sexton, and John Ashbery. Modern French people poets are Arthur Rimbaud and Charles Baudelaire. Rimbaud was famous for his poetry collection called “Illuminations.” Baudelaire wrote of beauty, decay, and spirituality in his famous work “Flowers of Evil.” Great Spanish people poets are Garcilaso de la Vega, Federico García Lorca, Antonio Machado, and Gustavo Adolfo Bécquer. Vicente Aleixandre won the Nobel Prize. Three Latin America Latin American poetry won the Nobel prize in the 20th century. They include Gabriela Mistral, Pablo Neruda, and Octavio Paz. They wrote in Spanish. Caribbean poets Derek Walcott and Giannina Braschi wrote epic poetry. Winner of the Nobel Prize, Walcott wrote ''Omeros'' and ''In a Green Night'' in English. Giannina Braschi writes epic poetry in English, Spanglish (language), and Spanish, including ''Empire of Dreams'' Ancient Chinese people poets are Wang Wei, Du Fu, and Qu Yuan.
