It is one of the first games to use 3D levels. The game is about killing demons and zombies in order to stop an invasion. ''Doom'' has traps, including crushing ceilings, radiation pools, and exploding barrels, that can kill the player. ''Doom'' was also the first game with multiplayer deathmatch (video games). This means you can fight with other players instead of the Demon and Zombie. The game was made with a computer language called C (programming language) using some new techniques created by John Carmack. ''Doom'' was the third big project for id Software, after ''Commander Keen'' (1990) and ''Wolfenstein 3D'' (1992). In May 1992, id started developing a darker game about fighting Demon, using a new 3D game engine from the lead programmer, John Carmack. The designer Tom Hall (game designer) at first wrote a science fiction plot, but he and most of the story were removed, with the final game featuring an Action movie-base design by John Romero and Sandy Petersen. Id published ''Doom'' three episodes under the shareware model, with the first episode free. A retail version with another episode was published in 1995 by GT Interactive called ''The Ultimate Doom''. ''Doom'' was a massive success, with it being called one of the best and most important video games of all time. It sold an 3.5 million copies by 1999. It has been called the "father" of First-person shooter and is called one of the most important games in the genre. It has been said by video game historians it changed the direction and public thought of video games as a whole. It led to many Ripoff, as well as having multiple Mod (video games) and and help start the speedrunning community. Its graphic violence led to controversy . ''Doom'' List of Doom ports to lots of platforms both officially and unofficially and the series got multiple sequels, including ''Doom 2'' (1994), ''Doom 64'' (1997), ''Doom 3'' (2004), ''Doom (2016 video game)'' (2016), ''Doom Eternal'' (2020), and ''Doom: The Dark Ages'' (2025), as well as some movies being made about Doom, ''Doom (movie)'' (2005) and ''Doom: Annihilation'' (2019). == Gameplay == File:Freedoom002 01.png ''Doom'' is a first-person shooter with 3D graphics. While levels are seen in a 3D view, the enemies and objects are instead 2D Sprites (computer graphics) rendered at fixed angles, this sometimes is referred to as 2.5D graphics or billboarding. In the single-player campaign mode, the player controls an unnamed Space Marines—later named "Doomguy"—goes through military bases on the moons of Mars and in hell. To finish a level, the player must traverse through levels to reach the exit room. Levels are grouped into named episodes, with the final level having a boss fight. While playing the levels, the player must fight enemies, including Demon and Spirit possession undead humans. Enemies are most the time in large groups. There are five difficulty levels this changes the number of enemies and amount of damage they do, on the hardest difficulty setting enemies moving and attacking faster than normal. The monsters work by move toward their opponent if they see or hear them, and attack by biting, clawing, or using magic like Fire. The player must use Medicine, for Health (video games) and armor. The player can find weapons and med-kits throughout the levels or can collect them from dead enemies, including a pistol, a shotgun, a chainsaw, a Weapons in science fiction, and the BFG 9000. The player also finds pits of toxic waste, ceilings that can crush objects, and locked doors requiring a keycard or a remote switch.Masters of Doom Power-up include health or armor, a mapping computer, Cloaking device, a Hazmat suit that helps with toxic waste. Cheating in video games allow the player to unlock all weapons, walk through walls, or become "god" and can't die. There are two Multiplayer video game modes that are playable over a network: cooperative, in the mode, two to four players team up to complete the main campaign, and Deathmatch (video games), in which two to four players compete to kill the other players.
