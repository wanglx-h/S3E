It is the capital of the province of Alicante (province). It is in the southern part of the Valencian Community. It is also a historic Mediterranean Sea port. The population of the city of Alicante proper is about 332,067, as of 2014, ranking as the second-largest Valencian Community city.[http://www.ine.es/en/inebmenu/mnu_cifraspob_en.htm Instituto Nacional de Estadística] Official population figures: Municipal Register. Including nearby municipalities, Alicante was populated by 458,843 residents. Population of the metropolitan area (including Elche and satellite towns) was 795,034 as of 2009. Based on population it is the eighth-largest List of metropolitan areas in Spain by population. The city is a sea Harbour and a tourism. It has a hot semi-arid climate (''BSh'' in the Koeppen climate classification). The avenue Maisonnave is the principal commercial street in the city of Alicante. In fact, Maisonnave is the sixth most expensive avenue in the whole of Spain, which gives an idea of the importance that this street has. The Corte inglés has in this central street both branches that it possesses in Alicant, one at each end of the avenue. Santa Barbara's Castle is 166 metres up the Benacantil mountain. It's very strong with great strategic value.
