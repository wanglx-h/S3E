Wikipedia:Internet History Sourcebooks Project During Napoleon I's reign, "Veillons au salut de l'Empire" (meaning "Let's Ensure the Salvation of the Empire") was the unofficial anthem of his reign, and under Napoleon III's control, it was "Partant pour la Syrie" (meaning "Leaving for Syria"). Eventually, the French government brought back "La Marseillaise" to try to spread patriotism to the French people during the Franco-Prussian War in 1870. A year later, after that war ended, "La Marseillaise" was adopted by the Paris Commune, although it had new lyrics under the title "La marseillaise de la Commune" (meaning "The Marseillaise of the Commune"). Eight years later, in 1879, it was one again the national anthem of France, and has remained so ever since. ==Lyrics== The lyrics mention about the time when France was being invaded by Prussia and Austria, while it was written. The popularity of this anthem has spread across the globe. It has been translated into several languages by known poets over the course of its existence. ====Basque variant==== The Basque language poet Joan Batista Arxu published a version in his 1848 collection ''Kantu patriotak'' (meaning "Patriotic songs"). ====Wallon variant==== The French national anthem has been the basis of a couple patriogic songs written in the Walloon language as part of the Walloon Movement.[https://wa.m.wikisource.org/wiki/Li_Mars%C3%A8y%C3%AAse_d%C3%A8s_Walons Â« La Marseillaise des Wallons Â» ratournaedje da Jean-Pol Pirson][https://wa.m.wikisource.org/wiki/Li_Marseyesse_e_walon Li Marseyesse e walon rortografiaedje da Lucyin Mahin] ===Other variants based on "La Marseillaise"=== There have been many different variants of the song, including: * "Belarusian Marseillaise", a Belarusian patriotic socialist song * "Deutsche Arbeiter-Marseillaise", a song created for the General German Worker's Association * "La Marseillaise de la Commune", the anthem of the Paris Commune in 1871 * "La Marseillaise des Blancs", the royalist and Catholic variation * "Onamo", a Montenegro patriotic song also known as the "Serbian Marseillaise" * "Brisbane Lions#Club song", the official team song of the Brisbane Lions * "The Internationale", a communism and socialism anthem * "The Women's Marseillaise", a women's suffrage song * "Worker's Marseillaise", a socialist song of the Russian Revolution ====Russian variant==== In Russia, "La Marseillaise" was used as a republican revolutionary anthem by Russian French speakers in the 18th century.
