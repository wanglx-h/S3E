It is a Goidelic languages, the same family as Scottish Gaelic language and Irish language. All of them are Celtic languages. Manx is spoken mainly by people who learn it through interest. It Extinct language as a natural community language in the 20th century. The last old native speaker died in 1974. Manx is protected under the European Charter for Regional or Minority Languages. == History == Manx began to separate from Middle Irish in about 900–1600 AD and is called ''Yn Ghaelg'' ''Yn Ghailck'' by its speakers. There became fewer and fewer speakers during the 19th century, when the language was replaced by English language. In the 1901 census, 9% of the people in the Isle of Man were said to speak Manx, but in the 1921 census, that had dropped to only 1%. Today, Manx is used as the only language taught at five of the Isle of Man’s preschools. Manx is taught as a second language at all of the Isle of Man's primary and secondary schools. == Today == Today, Manx is used as the only language taught at five of the Isle of Man’s preschools. Manx is taught as a second language at all of the Isle of Man's primary and secondary schools. There is now a school that teaches all of its lessons in Manx. According to the 2001 census, 2.2% of the population of the island could speak the language.
