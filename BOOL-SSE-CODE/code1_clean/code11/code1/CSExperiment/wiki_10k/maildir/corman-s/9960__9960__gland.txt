Subject: Gland
File:Tepegua-2-glands.jpg oil substance.]] A '''gland''' is a group of cells in the body which make substances such as hormones. They are in humans and other animals, and in plants. *If the substance is put directly into the bloodstream, the gland is called '''endocrine gland'''. *If the substance is carried by a duct, the gland is called an '''exocrine gland'''. Exocrine ducts empty outside the body, or into cavity inside the body. Examples of glands in humans are: *Sweat glands that produce sweat for perspiration. *The pineal gland in the brain, which produces the hormone melatonin at night. Category:Glands
