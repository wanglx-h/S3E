== Events == * May 15– Pope Innocent IV issues the papal bull ''Ad exstirpanda'', which allows the torture of heresy in the Medieval Inquisition. Torture is soon used widely across Catholicism Europe. * The first European gold coins are minted in the Italy city of Florence, and are known as florins. * The Poland Lubus Land is made a part of the German state of Brandenburg, beginning Brandenburg's expansion into previously Polish areas (Neumark). * The Sweden city of Stockholm is founded by Birger Jarl. * The Lithuanian city of Klaipėda is founded by the Teutonic Knights. * The town and monastery of Orval in Belgium burns to the ground; rebuilding takes 100 years. * Thomas Aquinas travels to the University of Paris to begin his studies there for a master's degree. * In astronomy, work begins on the recording of the Alfonsine tables.
