It is sometimes called '''Lowland Scots''' or '''Lallans'''. It is not ''Scottish English'' but the two are similar. Scottish English is a dialect of English and Scots is a separate language. Ulster Scots is a Dialect found in the north of Ireland. Scots is very different from the Scottish Gaelic language, which is a Celtic language. There have been disagreements about the linguistic, historical and social status of Scots. Focused broad Scots is at one end of a scale, with Scottish Standard English at the other.A.J. Aitken 1992. There was immigration of Old Norse language Middle English-speakers from the North and Midlands of England.A History of Scots to 1700, DOST Vol. 12 p. xliii Later influences on the development of Scots were from Romance languages via ecclesiastical and legal Latin, Norman languageA History of Scots to 1700, pp. lxiii-lxv and later Parisian French language from the Auld Alliance and Dutch language and Middle Low German influences by trade and immigration from the Low Countries.A History of Scots to 1700, pp. lxiii Scots also includes loanwords from contact with Gaelic. ===13-14th century=== From the 13th century, Early Scots spread further into Scotland through the burghs established by King David I of Scotland.
