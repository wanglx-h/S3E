Subject: Square kilometre
File:1 km2.svg A '''square kilometer''' (British English spelling ''square kilometre'', in short ''km²'') is a measurement of area. It is based on the International System of Units unit meter. == Equal areas == A square (geometry) that has a side length of 1 kilometre (1000 meters) has an area of one square kilometer. : 1,000 m × 1,000 m = 1 km × 1 km = 1 E6 m² = 1 km² There are 100 hectares in one square kilometer. One square kilometer is just less than 0.39 square miles. == Use == Square kilometer is often used to say how much surface something occupies on Earth. It can, for example, be used to describe the area of a city or List of countries by area. These usually take more than 1 km² on Earth. The number of people living in a square kilometer is known as population density. == Sources == Category:Units of area
