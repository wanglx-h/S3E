Subject: Hatred
'''Hatred''' is an emotion of very strong dislike for someone or something. It is a desire to avoid, restrict, remove, or destroy that person or thing. It is often thought the opposite of love. Hatred can also be a form of projection. Sigmund Freud said that hatred was a selfish feeling that wishes to get rid of whatever is making that person unhappy.Freud, S. (1915). The instincts and their vicissitudes. The ''Penguin Dictionary of Psychology'' says that hate is a "deep, enduring (long-lasting), intense emotion expressing wikt:hostility, anger, and wikt:hostility towards a person, group, or object."Reber, A.S., & Reber, E. (2002). ''The Penguin dictionary of psychology''. New York: Penguin Books.
