Subject: Anatolia
File:Map of the geographic region of Anatolia.png '''Anatolia''', also called '''Asia Minor''' (from Ancient Greek: 'Άνατολή, Turkish language '''Anadolu'''), is a peninsula at the west end of Asia. It forms the majority of modern Turkey. It has an area of . Most of it is a plateau. that averages above sea level. Its climate is continental climate and steppe in the interior, temperate along the northern coast, and subtropics along the southern and the western coasts. Most of its people speak Turkish language. Anatolia is surrounded by the Black Sea, the Aegean Sea and the Mediterranean Sea. It is between Europe and Asia. This has led to people of many different cultures living there. Many great History peoples have lived in or occupied Anatolia; They include the Hittites, Lydia, Ancient Greece, Persia, Armenians, Celts, Ancient Rome, Goths, Byzantine Empire, Seljuk Empire and Ottoman Empire. The Roman Empire had a province called Asia that was also called Phrygia. People later started to call the entire continent Asia. Because of this, the peninsula became called Asia Minor (Little Asia). Troy is the most famous of the many cities that existed long ago in Anatolia. Category:Middle East Category:Peninsulas of Asia
