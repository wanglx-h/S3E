It is the county seat (the city where the county is governed) of El Paso County, Texas. It is in the far southwestern part of the U.S. state of Texas, along Interstate 10. The name comes from "El Paso de Norte", meaning The Passageway to the North. The large majority of the city's inhabitants are Hispanic. The 2020 population from the United States Census Bureau was 678,815, making it the List of United States cities by population, the List of cities in Texas by population, and the second-largest city in the Southwestern United States behind Phoenix, Arizona. The city is also List of U.S. cities with large Hispanic populations in the United States, with 81% of its population being Hispanic. El Paso won the All-America City Award five times: 1969, 2010, 2018, 2020, and 2021. The city has a desert climate and is home to the University of Texas at El Paso.
