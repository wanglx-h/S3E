This was around 25% of the population: one in every four people.: "As best as can now be estimated, over two million Cambodians died during the 1970s because of the political events of the decade, the vast majority of them during the mere four years of the 'Khmer Rouge' regime. This court has tried some former Khmer Rouge leaders for crimes against humanity. Kaing Guek Eav – also known as Comrade Duch – was the first to be tried before the ECCC. Eav was the head of Security Prison 21 during the genocide. The court found him Guilt (law) of crimes against humanity and breaking the Geneva Conventions of 1949. He was eventually Sentence (law) to life imprisonment.
