Subject: Humphry Davy
File:Davy Humphry desk color Howard.jpg '''Sir Humphry Davy, 1st Baronet''', FRS (Fellow of the Royal Society) (born 17 December 1778 in Penzance, Cornwall, England; died 29 May 1829 in Geneva City, Switzerland), was a Cornish people chemist. His study of chemistry led to the first pure forms of some of the chemical elements such as water and sodium. ==References== Category:1778 births Category:1829 deaths Category:British chemists Category:People from Cornwall
