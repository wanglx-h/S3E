Biography, Paintings, Art, Flowers, & Facts |url=https://www.britannica.com/biography/Georgia-OKeeffe |website=Britannica |accessdate=4 August 2023 |language=en |date=22 June 2023}} She is known for her colorful close-up painting of flowers. She was born on November 15, 1887 on a farm in Sun Prairie, Wisconsin. O'Keeffe studied art at the Art Institute of Chicago and the Art Students League of New York. Georgia O'Keeffe married Alfred Stieglitz in 2010. Stieglitz was a famous photographer and he helped O'Keeffe gain success in her career. He owned the gallery which held her first solo exhibition. In 1929 O'Keeffe traveled to New Mexico. She was inspired by the desert and light. She moved there in 1949. In 1970 the Whitney Museum of American Art held a retrospective exhibition of her work. O’Keeffe died on March 6, 1986 in Santa Fe at the age of 98. == Auction record == O'Keefe holds the world auction record for a painting by a woman (November 2014). Her ''Jimson Weed/White Flower No 1'' sold for $44.4m (£28.8m) at the O'Keeffe Museum in Santa Fe, New Mexico. Georgia O'Keeffe painting sets auction record for female artist. ''BBC News''.
