File:Light switch inside explained.jpg A '''switch''' is something that changes the flux of an electrical circuit. The most common kind of '''switch''' is something (for example a railroad switch) which can be taken off of one course and put onto another. The term "switch" usually means electricity power. In applications where more than one switch is needed, (i.e. a telephone service) mechanical switches have been replaced by electronic switches which can be controlled automatically. The switch is called a "logic gate" when used in a mathematics form. In logic, arguments are represented as logic gates.
