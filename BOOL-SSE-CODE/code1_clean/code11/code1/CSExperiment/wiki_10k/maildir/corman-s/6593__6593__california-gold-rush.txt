The population boomed, and San Francisco became one of America's major cities. == Beginnings and growth == File:California Gold Rush handbill.jpg trips to California (1849)]] Before gold was discovered in 1848, California's population was around 160,000; most of these people were Native Americans in the United States. California became a state in 1850, and by about 1855, more than 300,000 people had arrived in California. In early 1848, a carpenter named James Wilson Marshall found small flakes of gold in a river while building a water-powered sawmill for John Sutter. He immediately went to tell Sutter, and they tried to keep the gold a secret.
