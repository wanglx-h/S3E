| image = McKellenRichmnd040219-5 (46275370484) (cropped).jpg | caption = McKellen in 2019 | birth_name = Ian Murray McKellen | birth_date = | birth_place = Burnley, Lancashire, England | occupation = Actor | years_active = 1958–present | partner = | education = St Catharine's College, Cambridge (Bachelor of Arts) | awards = | website = }} '''Sir Ian Murray McKellen''' (born 25 May 1939) is an English people actor. He has had a Tony Award and two Academy Awards nominations. ==Life== McKellen was born on 25 May 1939 in Burnley, Lancashire. In 1939, he moved to Wigan. In 1951, he moved to Bolton. His work has spanned genres from serious William Shakespeare and modern theatre to popular fantasy and science fiction movies. In a television movie role in 1970 titled ''Edward II'', he and his co-star, James Laurenson kissed in British television's first gay kiss. Best known for his roles as Gandalf in the 2001-2003 The Lord of the Rings movie trilogy, as Sir Leigh Teabing in ''The Da Vinci Code (movie)'', and as Magneto (comics) in the ''X-Men'' series of movies. He appeared in The Hobbit (movie series). He is coming out gay and is a prominent activist for the rights of LGBT people. He said that the reason why he took the role in the ''X-Men'' movies was because of his sexuality. He said: "Mutants are like gays. They’re cast out by society for no good reason". He was made a Order of the British Empire in 1979 and knighted in 1990 for his outstanding work and contributions to the theatre. In 2008 he was made a Companion of Honour. McKellen said in 2012 that he was diagnosed with prostate cancer in 2006. McKellen once signed an autograph to a fan stating "Fuck off, I’m gay."
