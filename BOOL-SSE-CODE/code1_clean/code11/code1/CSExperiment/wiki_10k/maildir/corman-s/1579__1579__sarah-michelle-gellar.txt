| birth_place = Long Island, New York, U.S. | other_names = Sarah Michelle Prinze | occupation = | years_active = 1981–present | spouse = | children = 2 | signature = }} '''Sarah Michelle Gellar''' (born April 14, 1977) is an Americans actress. After several television acting roles during the 1990s, Gellar became celebrity for playing Buffy Summers in the television programme ''Buffy the Vampire Slayer''. After ''Buffy'', she started acting in movies. She has starred in the family movies ''Scooby-Doo (movie)'' (2002) and ''Scooby-Doo 2: Monsters Unleashed'' (2004), the movie ''Harvard Man'' (2001), and the horror movies ''The Return'' (2006), ''The Grudge 2'' (2006), ''The Grudge'' (2004) and ''I Know What You Did Last Summer'' (1997). She won an award for her part in the 1999 movie ''Cruel Intentions''. == Early life == Gellar was born in New York City. She is the only child of Rosellen Greenfield and Arthur Gellar. Both of her parents were Judaism from Hungary and Russia, but Gellar's family had a Christmas tree during the holidays while she was growing up. In 1984, her parents divorced. She was raised by her mother on New York's Upper East Side. Gellar and her father did not have a good Interpersonal relationship from this time until his death from liver cancer on October 9, 2001. She went to school at New York City's Columbia Grammar & Preparatory School and the Professional Children's School. Gellar was a very good student and was a good figure skater. Her best friend was Melissa Joan Hart. Several years later, Hart was the star of the series ''Clarissa Explains It All'' and ''Sabrina, the Teenage Witch (TV series)''. == Television career == File:Sarah Michelle Gellar.jpg International Film Festival.]] At the age of four, Gellar was seen by an talent agent in a restaurant in Uptown Manhattan. Two weeks later, she auditioned for a part in ''An Invasion Of Privacy'', a movie made for television. The movie starred Valerie Harper, Carol Kane and Jeff Daniels. At the audition, Gellar read the lines for both her own fictional character and Harper's character. The film director thought this was very impressive and she got the job. A short while later, she got a part in a television advertisement for the fast food restaurant Burger King. In it, she said bad things about McDonald's and said she only eating at Burger King. Gellar made other commercials while doing other acting job. Some of these jobs were playing Emily in an episode of the TV series ''Spenser: For Hire'', appearing in a small role in the Chevy Chase comedy movie ''Funny Farm (movie)'' and in the movie ''High Stakes (movie)''. She was also in Europe working on the TV series ''Crossbow (TV series)''. In 1991, she played a young Jacqueline Kennedy Onassis in ''A Woman Named Jackie''. File:Sarah M Gellar.jpg Gellar's major break came in 1992. She starred in the teenager soap opera ''Swan's Crossing''. After that, she got a part in another soap opera, ''All My Children''. She playing the character Kendall Hart, the long-lost daughter of character Erica Kane (Susan Lucci). In 1995, at the age of eighteen, she won a Daytime Emmy Award for Outstanding Younger Leading Actress in a Drama Series for her work as Kendall Hart. Gellar left ''All My Children'' in 1995. People were saying that her and Susan Lucci did not get along and this caused problems with them working together. She got the main role in the 1997 Television series ''Buffy the Vampire Slayer''. She played a teenager who had to fight many of supernatural enemy with the help of the Scooby Gang and her Watcher (btvs) (a mentor). The show was well liked by both the critics and the audience. Another series, (''Angel (TV series)''), was made based on characters from ''Buffy''. ''Buffy'' was on television for seven seasons and a total of 144 episodes. During the show, Buffy, and Gellar also, became cultural icons in the United States and the United Kingdom. They were a symbol of "empowered" women. Gellar also sang several of the songs during the ''Buffy the Vampire Slayer'' Musical theater episode "Once More, with Feeling (Buffy episode)". During the show's later years, Gellar said she did not like some parts of season six and the seventh season's final episode. After the show ended, Gellar said that she did not want to be in a ''Buffy'' movie. Since that time, she has said she might do it if the screenplay is good enough. Gellar's picture has been used on the covers of ''Cosmopolitan (magazine), Glamour (magazine), FHM, Rolling Stone'', and other magazines. She was part of Maxim (magazine) ''Hot 100'' list in 2002, 2003, and 2005, and in FHM ''100 Sexiest Women'' of 2005. In 1998, she was named one of ''People (magazine)'' Magazine's "50 Most Beautiful People (in the World)". Gellar has also been in the Stone Temple Pilots music video ''Sour Girl'' and is a celebrity spokesperson for the cosmetics company, Maybelline. == Movie career == After ''Buffy'', Gellar tried to use her television fame for a movie career. Some of her movies were well liked, others were not. After roles in the Popularity horror movies ''I Know What You Did Last Summer'' and ''Scream 2'' (both 1997), she starred in the 1999 movies ''Simply Irresistible'', a romantic comedy, and ''Cruel Intentions''. ''Cruel Intentions'' was a small hit at the box office. It made over $38 million in the United States. The movie had a lesbian kiss between Gellar and co-star Selma Blair that won the "Best Kiss" award at the 2000 MTV Movie Awards. film critic Roger Ebert said that Gellar is "effective as a bright girl who knows exactly how to use her act as a tramp". Gellar starred as Daphne in the box office success ''Scooby-Doo (movie)'' (2002), a live-action version of the cartoon series. Gellar, Prinze and their friends Matthew Lillard and Linda Cardellini were also in the sequel ''Scooby-Doo 2: Monsters Unleashed'' (2004), which made less money than the first movie. Gellar's next movie was the 2004 horror movie ''The Grudge''. It was very well liked. Gellar appeared in the sequel ''The Grudge 2''. It opened on October 13, 2006. She has also starred in several movies that have yet to be released, including ''Southland Tales'', ''The Air I Breathe'' and ''The Girls' Guide to Hunting And Fishing''. == Personal life == File:Freddie Prinze Jr and Sarah Michelle Gellar by David Shankbone.jpg at the 2007 Tribeca Film Festival]] Gellar met her husband Freddie Prinze, Jr. during filming of the 1997 teen horror movie ''I Know What You Did Last Summer'' but the two did not begin a intimate relationship until 2000.
