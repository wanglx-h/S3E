He is most famous for his ''The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy'' series. Adams was born in Cambridge. When he was a few months old he moved to East London and a few years later to Brentwood, Essex. He originally received attention when he wrote for the popular television shows ''Monty Python'' and ''Doctor Who'' in the 1970s. In 1978 he wrote a Science fiction Radio play called ''The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy''. It was later turned into a novel. In the early 1980s, it became a television series and in 2005 it became a movie produced by The Walt Disney Company. Adams also wrote several sequels for radio and as novels. He was also known for his humorous detective novels starring Dirk Gently, and for his efforts to conserve endangered species.
