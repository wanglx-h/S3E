Assassinations are usually done for political reasons or contract killing. A person who '''assassinates''' someone is called an '''assassin'''. Throughout history, assassinations have happened for many different reasons. Sometimes, assassinations have been used to coup d'etat. Other times, assassinations have been used to kill military leaders during wars, or for religious reasons. Some assassins want revenge, or just want to be wikt:famous. ==In ancient to medieval times== Assassinations have happened ever since people started living in group society. The study showed that: * Since 1895, assassins have tried to kill national leaders 298 different times. 59 of those world leaders were killed. * Since 1950, a national leader has been assassinated in nearly two out of every three years. Another study looked at all assassinations between 1946 and 2013. It included not just national leaders, but also other members of government (including local governments), and people who were against the government. This study found that between 1946 and 2013, a total of 954 people were assassinated in 758 different attacks. This study also looked at who was most likely to be assassinated.
