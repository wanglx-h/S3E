It is the capital (city) of the state of Tasmania, and it has about 200,000 people. It is on the western shore of the Derwent River, although some of its suburbs are on the eastern shore. In the western suburbs of Hobart is Mount Wellington. Hobart was founded in 1803 as a penal colony. Today it is a busy seaport, and is the home port of Antarctica expeditions. Tourist attractions include Salamanca Markets, the Cadbury chocolate factory and the Cascade Brewery.
