|related comps=FIFA World Cup |region=International |number of teams= |current champions= (1st title) (2023) |most successful team= (4 titles)}} |broadcasters=2023 FIFA Women's World Cup#Broadcasting rights |website= |current=}} The '''FIFA Women's World Cup''' is a Football for List of women's national association football teams from countries that are part of FIFA. It happens every four years, one year after the men's World Cup. The first one was in China in 1991 FIFA Women's World Cup. Teams compete for 31 spots through qualifications, and the host country gets the 32nd spot. The tournament, called the World Cup Finals, takes place in the host country over about a month. Five different countries have won the nine Women's World Cup tournaments. The United States women's national soccer team won four times, Germany women's national football team twice, and Japan women's national football team, Norway women's national football team, and Spain women's national football team once each. Eight countries have hosted the Women's World Cup, including China and the United States twice. Australia, Canada, France, Germany, New Zealand, and Sweden have each hosted it once.
