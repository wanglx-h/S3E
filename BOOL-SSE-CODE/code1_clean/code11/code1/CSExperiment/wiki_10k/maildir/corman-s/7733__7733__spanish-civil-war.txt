The war ended on 1 April 1939, when the last Republican troops surrendered. Francisco Franco became dictator of Francoist Spain until he died in 1975. The civil war began after a ''pronunciamiento'' (revolt) against the Second Spanish Republic by the Nationalist faction (Spanish Civil War). The nationalists were supported by FE de las JONS, a Far-right politics political party. During this time, Fascism General Francisco Franco and his nationalist troops wanted to take control of the Spain. The Army split, with about half of the troops being loyal to the Republic. Many different groups worked together to help the Democratic republic Second Spanish Republic stop Franco, including Basque people, Catalans and other groups. The most famous atrocity was the bombing of Guernica. On 26 April 1937, the city was bombed by Legion Condor, a Nazi Germany military unit working for Franco. It was the first time that an air bombing caused so many civilian casualties. The bombing was reported all over the world in newspapers and made people aware of the German involvement in the Spanish Civil War.
