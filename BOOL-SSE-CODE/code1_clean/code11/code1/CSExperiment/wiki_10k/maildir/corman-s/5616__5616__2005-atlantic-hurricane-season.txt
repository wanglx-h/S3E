Subject: 2005 Atlantic hurricane season
The '''2005 Atlantic hurricane season''' was the time from June 1 to November 30, 2005 when hurricanes officially formed in the Atlantic Ocean. Storms sometimes form before and after these dates but most storms form during the season. Tropical Storm Zeta formed on December 30 and dissipated on January 6, which is after the November 30 end of the season. It was the most active Atlantic hurricane season in recorded history. 2005 broke many records set. There were 28 storms that were tropical storms, which was broken by the 2020 Atlantic hurricane season. There were 15 storms that became hurricanes. For the first time ever storms were named by the Greek alphabet as the normal name list had all been used up. The storm which caused the most damage was Hurricane Katrina, which hit New Orleans, Louisiana, USA, and killed over 1000 people. The strongest storm of the season was not Katrina, it was Hurricane Wilma.
