calling_code = +963 | iso3166code = SY | cctld = .syسوريا. | demonym = Syrian | today = | ethnic_groups_year = 2014 }} '''Syria''' is a country in the Middle East, the west part of Asia. It borders Turkey, Lebanon, Jordan, Iraq and Palestine (region). Its western part faces the Mediterranean Sea and shares a maritime border with Cyprus, while its eastern and northern parts are Mountain. Syria's national Capital (city) is Damascus. The biggest city is Aleppo. It has been wikt:embroil in Syrian civil war since March 2011. The religious makeup of Syria is 74% Sunni, 11% Alawite, 10% Christianity, 3% Druze, 1% Ismaili 0.4% Twelvers and 0.1% Yazidis. ==History== ===Before 20th century=== Syria has a very long History of Asia. It was a land of Phoenicia, which then became part of the Achaemenid Empire, Roman Empire, and then the Eastern Roman Empire. In those days people in Syria spoke the Syriac language and used the Syriac alphabet, while the Syrian city Antioch was one of the important cities in Christendom. ====Islamization==== The Rashidun Caliphate took over Syria from the Eastern Roman Empire in the 6th century. Most of the indigenous population converted to Islam and adopted the Arabic language. The region then became an important province of the subsequent Muslim empires under the name of ''Bilād ash-Shām''. * * * * * ===20th century=== When World War I started, the Ottoman Empire ruled Syria and many other places. When it ended, France controlled Lebanon and Syria. British Empire had Iraq, Jordan and Palestine. They drew a border between Iraq and Syria in 1920. France controlled Syria until 1946 when Syria became its own country. ====Cold War==== File:Report of the Commission Entrusted by the Council with the Study of the Frontier between Syria and Iraq Listen to this page.png drawn in 1920 separating Iraq and Syria.]]Syria was part of the United Arab Republic with Egypt between 1958 and 1961. Syria fought some wars with Israel. Its former territory Golan Heights has been governed by Israel since Six-Day War. * * * Meanwhile, the Ba'athism seized power with a Coup d'état in 1963 to establish a junta. * * * * * * * * * * In 1970, Hafiz al-Assad, the father of Bashar al-Assad, took over with another coup and turned the Ba'athist Syrian state into a Dynasty Totalitarianism state, featured by systematic oppression and widespread Persecution, which was passed on to Bashar when Hafiz passed away in 2000. ===21st century=== Most Syrians believe in Islam, while an ethnoreligious Christian minority, called the Syriac Christians, exists. * * * * * ====2010s==== In 2011, anti-Tyrant Arab Spring#Events were brutally suppressed by President Bashar al-Assad, sparking off a Syrian civil war against Assad. Assad went on to commit countless wikt:atrocity against Syrians, including Genocidal massacre, Famine Siege * * * * * and Poison gas attacks, * * * * * * * * * * which killed over 400,000 Syrians. Amid the war, the Kurdish people took over a wikt:sizeable part of northern Syria, wikt:namely the The Autonomous Administration of North and East Syria. * * * * * In 2016, Assad regained most of Syria with help from military forces from Russia and Iran. * * * * * ====2020s==== A myriad of factors, including the COVID-19 pandemic * * * and pro-Russian Western leftist opposition to North Atlantic Treaty Organization intervention, * * * * * * * * * * had made the civil war a frozen conflict for several years until December 2024. * * * * * In December 2024, regrouped rebel forces launched large-scale multi-pronged offensives amid weakened local Russian and Iranian military presence, * * * * * reportedly due to the countries' respective involvement in the Russian invasion of Ukraine and Israel–Hamas war. Aleppo, Homs and Daraa, * * * * * three of the largest Syrian cities, fell to the rebels within a week, who also wikt:encircle Syria's capital Damascus. * * * * * ===Fall of Ba'athist rule=== On December 8, 2024, Bashar al-Assad fled the capital as the rebels stormed in. * * * * * Assad's Protectorate Russia claimed that Assad had left Syria * * * * * for Moscow. * * * * * Rebels inside the capital announced the end of the six-decade Ba'athist rule in Syria. * * * * * ==Geography== Syria is between latitudes 32° and 38° N, and longitudes 35° and 43° E. It is mostly arid plateau. The area bordering the Mediterranean is fairly green. The Euphrates, Syria's most important river, crosses the country in the east. The climate in Syria is dry and hot. Winters are mild. ==Politics and government== The Syrian transitional government has been in control, since March 2025. * * * * * In March 2025, the 2012 constitution was set aside, and a new interim constitution came in its place. Syria is a republic. A Constitution of Syria was started on 13 March 1971, making Syria a ''wikt:de jure'' secular socialist state. Islam was the majority religion. A new constitution came into place in 2012. ===Branches of government=== The executive branch is the President of Syria, two Vice president, the prime minister, and the Council of Ministers. The constitution says the president must be a Muslim. It does not make Islam the state religion. According to the 2012 constitution, the president is elected by the Syrian people in a direct election. The People's Council is the legislative branch. ====State control==== Nearly all of Syria’s radio and television outlets are State-owned enterprise. The Ba'ath Party controls nearly all newspapers. ====Human rights==== Syria's human rights are among the worst in the world, according to human rights organizations such as Human Rights Watch. The authorities kidnap democracy and human rights activists, Censorship websites and impose travel bans on their targets. ===Military=== The President of Syria is commander in chief of the Syrian armed forces. There are about 400,000 troops. Ethnic Kurds have their own army called YPG. The Males must go in the military when they are age 18.[http://www.globalsecurity.org/military/world/syria/overview.htm Syria – Overview]. Retrieved 2010-02-01. The armed forces stood down with the fall of the Ba'athist rule on December 8, 2024. ===Governorates=== Syria has fourteen Governorates, or ''muhafazat''. The governorates are divided into sixty districts. The economy is based on agriculture, oil, industry and tourism. ===Transport=== Syria has three international airports (Damascus, Aleppo and Lattakia). They are hubs for Syrian Air. Foreign airlines also fly to them.
