Subject: On the Waterfront
'''''On the Waterfront''''' is a 1954 United States movie about wikt:mob violence among longshoreman (people who unload ships) in New Jersey. Directed by Elia Kazan, it stars Marlon Brando, Eva Marie Saint and Karl Malden. The movie won eight Academy Awards: for Best Picture, Best Actor (Brando), Best Supporting Actor (Saint), Academy Award for Best Production Design, Best Director (Kazan), Best Cinematography (Boris Kaufman), Best Film Editing (Gene Milford) and Best Original Screenpaly (Budd Schulberg). The catchphrase, "I could've been a contender", is said by Terry Malloy. In 2005, it ranked third on American Film Institute's list of the AFI's 100 Years... 100 Movie Quotes [http://www.abc.net.au/news/newsitems/200506/s1398449.htm].
