Green: areas with a notable minority of Romanian speakers. }} File:Ro-Ion Paladi.ogg The '''Romanian language''' is a Romance language, meaning it comes from Latin like French language, Spanish language and Italian language. It has 66% Latin-based words and around 20-25% Slavic languages-based words. The rest are newer and come from Dacian, Turkish language, Greek language, or English language. There are about 28 million speakers: 24 million who speak it as their mother tongue and 4 million who have learned it as their second language. Romanian is also the most spoken language in Moldova, which is northeast of Romania. The Moldovan language is a kind of Romanian with certain differences, such as the dialect and a Moldavian accent. ==References== Category:Languages of Romania Category:Romance languages Category:Languages of Moldova
