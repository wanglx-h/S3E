Image:ChristopherWalkenFeb08.jpg '''Ronald Walken''' (born March 31, 1943), simply known as '''Christopher Walken''', is an Americans movie, television, and theatre actor. He is best known for playing evil or mind damaged fictional character. He has sometimes used that image for comedy effect. He was born in Queens, New York to a Germany father and Scotland-born mother. Walken has been married to casting director Georgianne Walken since 1969. Walken is a skilled dancer who in many of his movies dances, because he requests it. He has been in nearly one hundred movies and television shows since 1953, including ''The Dead Zone (movie)'' (1983 in movies), ''Brainstorm (1983 movie)'' (1983), ''A View to a Kill'' (1985 in movies), ''Batman Returns'' (1992 in movies) ''True Romance'' (1993 in movies), ''Pulp Fiction'' (1994 in movies), ''Nick of Time (movie)'' (1995 in movies), ''Mousehunt'', (1997 in movies), Catch Me If You Can'' (2002), ''Around the Bend'' (2004) and ''The Jungle Book (2016 movie)'' (2016). He was George Lucas' second choice for Han Solo after Harrison Ford. He won the Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor in ''The Deer Hunter'' (1978) where he played a disturbed Vietnam war veteran with Robert De Niro. He was nominated again in 2002 for ''Catch Me if You Can''. Walken has been guest host on Saturday Night Live many times.
