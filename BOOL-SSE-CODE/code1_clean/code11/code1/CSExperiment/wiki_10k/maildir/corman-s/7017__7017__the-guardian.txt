Subject: The Guardian
File:The Guardian 2018.svg '''The Guardian''' is a British newspaper. It is published every day, except on Sunday. It is owned by the same company as '''The Observer''', which only publishes on Sunday. ''The Guardian'' is edited by Katharine Viner. == History == File:GuardianNewsroom.jpg]] The newspaper was founded in 1821. It was founded by textile traders and merchants. The Guardian was seen as "an organ of the middle class",Friedrich Engels, ''The Condition of the Working Class in England'', Progress, 1973, p 109 or, in the words of C.P. Scott's son Ted, "a paper that will remain bourgeoisie to the last".Ayerst, ''The Guardian'', 1971, p.471 In 2005, Sir Max Hastings stated that he writes for ''The Guardian'' "because it is read by the new establishment", reflecting the paper's growing influence. Until 1959 ''The Guardian'' was called ''The Manchester Guardian''. Three of the four people who wrote leaders for the ''Guardian'' joined the Liberal Democrats on its foundation in 1982. The paper supported Tony Blair when he wanted to lead the Labour Party''Guardian'' leader, 2 July 1994 and to become Prime Minister.''Guardian'' leader, 2 May 1997 In 1999 [http://theguardian.com theguardian.com] started.
