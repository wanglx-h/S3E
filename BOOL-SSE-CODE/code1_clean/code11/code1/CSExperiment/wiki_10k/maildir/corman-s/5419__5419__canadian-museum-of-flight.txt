Subject: Canadian Museum of Flight
The '''Canadian Museum of Flight''' is an aircraft museum at the Langley Regional Airport in Langley, British Columbia (district municipality), British Columbia, Canada. The museum has about 20 peace-time and war-time motor airplanes, gliders, and helicopters to see, six of which can still fly. ==Aircraft== List of aircraft displayed : * Avro Canada Avro CF-100 Canuck Mk.3B * Beechcraft Beechcraft Model 18 (C-45) * Bowlus Bumblebee BB1 * Canadair (Lockheed) T-33 Shooting Star * Canadair Canadair CL-41 Tutor (used by the Snowbirds) * Rutan Quickie (Homebuilt) * Conair Firecat * Douglas DC-3 "CF-PWH" "Spirit of the Skeena" * De Havilland Vampire * de Havilland De Havilland Tiger Moth (Flying condition) * Fleet 16B Fleet Finch (Flying condition) * Handley Page Hampden * Lockheed Canadair CF-104 * North American T-6 Texan (Flying condition) * Mignet Flying Flea * Royal Aircraft Factory S.E.5a (7/8 Replica) (Flying condition) * Sikorsky H-19 Chickasaw * Sopwith Camel (Duplicate) * Struchen Ultralight Helicopter * Waco AQC-6 (Flying condition) * Waco INF (Flying condition) * Westland Lysander Mk III * == Other websites == *[http://www.canadianflight.org/ Canadian Museum of Flight] *[http://www.canadianflight.org/ Canadian Museum of Flight website] ==References== Category:Aircraft museums Category:Museums in Canada Category:Organizations based in British Columbia
