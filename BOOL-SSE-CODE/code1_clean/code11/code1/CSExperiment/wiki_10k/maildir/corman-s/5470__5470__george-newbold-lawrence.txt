Subject: George Newbold Lawrence
File:George Newbold Lawrence.jpg '''George Newbold Lawrence''' (October 20, 1806 - January 17, 1895) was an Americans businessperson and amateur ornithologist (bird expert). Lawrence counted birds for Spencer Fullerton Baird and John Cassin, and the three men wrote ''Birds of North America'' in 1858. Lawrence left his 8,000 bird skins to the American Museum of Natural History. The Lawrence's Goldfinch ''Carduelis lawrencei'' was named for him by Cassin.
