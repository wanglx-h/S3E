Often called "The Don", he is widely acknowledged as one of the greatest batting (cricket)s of all time. He scored 6,996 Scoring (cricket)#Runs in his Test cricket career at a world record batting average of 99.94 runs per completed innings. ==Playing career== Bradman made his first-class cricket in the 1927–28 season, playing for the New South Wales cricket team state team. He made his Test cricket in November 1928, playing for Australia men's national cricket team against England men's cricket team at the Brisbane Cricket Ground. His Test career continued until August 1948, when he made his final appearance at The Oval. ==Personal life== Bradman married his childhood sweetheart Jessie Martha Menzies on 30 April 1932, and they were together for 65 years until Jessie died in 1997. Bradman said on many occasions how important his wife was to him, once saying: "I would never have achieved what I achieved without Jessie". Don and Jessie Bradman had three children, but family life was difficult. Their first child died young in 1936. Their second child (John, born in 1939) caught poliomyelitis, a serious virus disease, and their third child (Shirley, born 1941) had multiple sclerosis. ==Death== Bradman died of pneumonia on 25 February 2001, aged 92, at his home in Kensington Park, Adelaide. ==References== ==Bibliography== * Category:1908 births Category:2001 deaths Category:Australian cricketers Category:Knights Bachelor Category:Sportspeople from New South Wales Category:Order of Australia Category:Australian sport administrators
