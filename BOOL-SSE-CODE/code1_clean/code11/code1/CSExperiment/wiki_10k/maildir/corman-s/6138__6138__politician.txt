Subject: Politician
File:London_Summit_2009-1.jpg]] A '''politician''' (from Ancient Greek πόλις, "polis") can be anyone who seeks to get power in any Bureaucracy institution. It is most used for a person active in Political party politics, or a person holding or seeking office in government. In Democracy countries, politicians seek Election within a government through elections or, at times, temporary appointment to replace politicians who have died, resignation or have been otherwise removed from office. In non-democratic countries, they employ other means of reaching power through appointment, bribery, revolutions and intrigues. Some politicians are experienced in the art or science of government. someone who is active in government usually as an elected official|url = http://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/politician|website = www.merriam-webster.com|accessdate = 2015-10-02}} Politicians propose, support and create Law or Policy that govern the land and, by extension, its people. The word politician is sometimes replaced with the word statesman. Politicians have always used language, as in speeches or campaign advertisements. They use common wikt:themes to develop their political positions in terms familiar to the voters.Jonathan Charteris-Black, ''Politicians and rhetoric: The persuasive power of metaphor'' (Palgrave-MacMillan, 2005) Politicians become experts at using the media Ofer Feldman, ''Beyond public speech and symbols: Explorations in the rhetoric of politicians and the media'' (2000). With the rise of mass media in the 19th century they made heavy use of newspapers, magazines, and pamphlets, as well as posters.Robert J.
