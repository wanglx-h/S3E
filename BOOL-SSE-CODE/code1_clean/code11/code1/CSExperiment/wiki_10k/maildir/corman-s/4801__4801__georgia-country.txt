'''Georgia''' is a country in the South Caucasus region of Western Asia and Eastern Europe. It is situated on the coast of the Black Sea and borders Russia to the north, Turkey and Armenia to the south, and Azerbaijan to the southeast. It is largely encircled by the Greater and Lesser Caucasus mountain ranges. During 1918–1921, and 1991–1995, its full name was the '''Republic of Georgia'''. Since 1995, it has been Georgia, as written in its constitution. It was part of the Russian Empire and later Soviet Union between 1921 and 1991, but now it is an Independence republic. The capital city is '''Tbilisi'''. Almost 1.28 million people live there. "Population by regions". National Statistics Office of Georgia. Then, in 1798 the Persian people burned Tbilisi to the ground. From 1811 to 1918, Georgia was under the Tsar of Russia. Their culture survived intact.
