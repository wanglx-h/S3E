Definition, History, Examples, Importance, & Facts |url=https://www.britannica.com/science/zoology |date=October 5, 2024 |access-date=December 3, 2024}} '''Zoologists''' are scientists who study animals. They may work in Laboratory, or do field research. The methods are many and various. At the heart, they cover the structure, Function (biology), ecology and evolution of animals. The structure is investigated by dissection, and Microscope examination. The function is investigated by observation and experiment. Palaeontology supplies information about Extinction animals.
