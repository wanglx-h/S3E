File:James Brown 2001.jpg '''James Brown''' (May 3, 1933 â€“ December 25, 2006) was an Americans R&B and funk singer. He was known as the "Godfather of Soul". He was born in Barnwell, South Carolina, South Carolina and grew up in Augusta, Georgia, Georgia (U.S. state). Brown was arrested by police on many occasions. When he was 16, he was convicted of armed robbery, for which he spent three years in a youth detention center.[https://www.rollingstone.com/music/artists/james-brown/biography Rolling Stone] His other convictions include assaults. Four of Brown's albums were in ''Rolling Stone'' magazine's 2003 list of the 500 greatest albums of all time. Brown's most well known song is "Get Up (I Feel Like Being a) Sex Machine". In 2006, Brown died of pneumonia and heart disease in Atlanta. There was a public memorial service at Apollo Theater. Michael Jackson, Stevie Wonder, and Prince were there.
