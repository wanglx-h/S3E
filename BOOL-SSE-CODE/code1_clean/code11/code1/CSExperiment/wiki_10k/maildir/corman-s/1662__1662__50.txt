'''50''' (Roman numerals: '''L''') is a common year of the Gregorian calendar. It started on a Saturday. It was a common year starting on Thursday of the Julian calendar. The Gregorian calendar was not in use at the time of the common year starting on Saturday and ending on Saturday. It is one of only seven years to use just one Roman numeral. The seven are 1 (I), 5 (V), 10 (X), '''50 AD''' (L), 100 (C), 500 (D), and 1000 (M). == Events == * The Romans create a city called London on the River Thames == Deaths == * Abgarus of Edessa, king of Osroene * Aulus Cornelius Celsus, writer of ''De Medicina'' * Gamliel I, the nasi of the Jewish people in Babylonia Category:50
