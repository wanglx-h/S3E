Subject: Initialism
An '''initialism''' is a word made from the first letters of each word in a phrase. Unlike acronyms, initialisms cannot be spoken as words: they are spoken letter by letter. Examples of initialisms are: *DVD - Digital Versatile Disc *Compact disc - Compact Disc *Automated teller machine - Automated (or Automatic) Teller Machine *BRB - Be Right Back *NMHU - Not Much Here, You? *GTG/G2G - Got To Go *JW - Just Wondering *JK - Just Kidding *OK - Okay, "orl korrekt", misspelling of "all correct".
