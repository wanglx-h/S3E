Subject: EastEnders
File:EastEnders.svg '''''EastEnders''''' is a soap opera in the United Kingdom. It has been running since 19th February 1985, and has had over 7000 episodes. The programme is a rival of ''Coronation Street'' and ''Emmerdale'' on ITV. It is shown on BBC One. File:The Queen Vic.jpg Pub]] The programme is in a pretend area of East London, called Walford. It is filmed in Elstree, a village in Borehamwood. Like ''Coronation Street'', most action is around a local pub. The pub in ''EastEnders'' is called The Queen Vic. It quickly became very popular when it launched and had its highest rated episode on 25th December 1986 (Christmas Day) when Den Watts served his wife Angie divorce papers behind the Queen Vic which was watched by 30 million viewers, making it one of the most watched shows in the UK of all time. There are many storylines, including Illegal drugs, rape, racism, domestic violence, murder, kidnapping, prostitution, theft, dementia, arson, and runaway children. Arguments between members of one family, or between different families, are often in the storylines.
