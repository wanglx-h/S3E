Image:Elizabeth Gaskell.jpg '''Elizabeth Cleghorn Gaskell''' (29 September 1810 – 12 November 1865)[http://www.bbc.co.uk/history/historic_figures/gaskell_elizabeth.shtml bbc.co.uk] was an English people writer. She was born in Chelsea, London. After her mother died, she lived with her aunt in Knutsford, Cheshire. She married William Gaskell, a priest, in 1832. They lived in Manchester, where several of her books are set. She died of a heart attack in Holybourne, Hampshire. Her most famous books are ''Mary Barton'', ''Cranford'', ''North and South (Gaskell)'', and ''Wives and Daughters''. She also wrote a book of the life of her friend, writer Charlotte Brontë.
