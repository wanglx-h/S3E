File:Talleyrand 01.jpg]] '''Charles Maurice de Talleyrand-Périgord''' (2 February 1754 – 17 May 1838) was a French people diplomat. He was a bishop and worked successfully for Louis XVI of France. He changed sides and served the French Revolution and Napoleon I of France. When Napoleon started losing his wars, Talleyrand changed sides again and served Louis XVIII of France. He represented France at the Congress of Vienna. He changed sides once more and served Louis Philippe I. Since the beginning of the 19th century he was known simply as ''Talleyrand''. He is widely seen as one of the most pragmatic and influential diplomats in European history.
