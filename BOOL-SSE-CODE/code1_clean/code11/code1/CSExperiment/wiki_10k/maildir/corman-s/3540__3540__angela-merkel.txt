| birth_place = Hamburg, West Germany (Germany since 1990) | death_date = | death_place = | party = Christian Democratic Union (Germany) (1990–present) | otherparty = Democratic Awakening (1989–1990) | spouse = Ulrich Merkel (1977–1982)Joachim Sauer (1998–present) | alma_mater = University of Leipzig | profession = Physical chemist | signature = Angela Merkel Signature.svg }} '''Angela Dorothea Merkel''' (born ''Angela Dorothea Kasner'' on 17 July 1954 in Hamburg) is a Germans politician, and was Chancellor of Germany from 22 November 2005 to 8 December 2021. She was born in Hamburg, at the time part of West Germany, and grew up in the former German Democratic Republic, where she lived until the German reunification in 1990. Merkel is now married to Joachim Sauer, a professor of chemistry. From 2000 to 2018, she was the chairperson (was in charge of) the German conservative party, the Christian Democratic Union of Germany (CDU). Merkel became Germany's first female Chancellor of Germany on 22 November 2005 in a coalition of the two biggest German Political parties in Germany, the CDU/Christian Social Union of Bavaria and SPD. After the next election in 2009, she formed a coalition with the German liberal party FDP. After the 2013 and 2017 Federal Elections, she again emerged as chancellor of a coalition government. Her father was a Lutheranism pastor and her mother was a school teacher. Before becoming a politician, she worked as a researcher in Physical chemistry. In December 2015, she was named as ''Time'' magazine's Time Person of the Year. In October 2018, Merkel announced her permanent retirement from politics, as she said she would not seek reelection as leader of the CDU at the party convention in Christian Democratic Union of Germany leadership election, 2018 and as Chancellor in Next German federal election.
