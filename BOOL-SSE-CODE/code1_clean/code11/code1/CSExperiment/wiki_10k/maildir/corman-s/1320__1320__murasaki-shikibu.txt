She died in Kyoto, around the year 1020. == ''The Tale of Genji'' == ''The Tale of Genji'' is split into 54 different books, and is made up of 795 different poems. It is one of the earliest novels in Japanese language, and is often thought of as the first novel in the world. It is a classic of Japanese literature. The masterpiece was about the court life of Prince Genji, and his wife, Aoi in 10th-century Japan. Prince Genji was the Legitimacy son of an Emperor of Japan, and the text is about his relationships in court.
