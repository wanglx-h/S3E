Subject: Ocellated turkey
| image = Meleagris ocellata1.jpg | genus = Meleagris | species = ocellata | authority = Georges Cuvier, 1820 }} An '''ocellated turkey''' (''Meleagris ocellata'') is a turkey (bird) (a large bird) that is named for the eye-like spots on the tail feathers (''ocellated'' means having eye-like spots). It is similar to the wild turkey that is found more to the north in North America. ==References== Category:Turkeys Category:Birds of North America
