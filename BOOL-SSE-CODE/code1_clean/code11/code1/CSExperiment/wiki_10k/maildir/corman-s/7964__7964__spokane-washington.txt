Spokane is in the eastern half of the state, about from Idaho. Spokane is south of Canada. Spokane is the second largest city in Washington, while Seattle, Washington is the largest. Spokane's nickname is the "Lilac City" because of how many of those flowers grow in the area. The population of the city in 2020 was 228,989, and the population of the metro area of Spokane is 585,784. ==History== Spokane was founded in 1871 when a sawmill was built on the Spokane Falls. In 1889 a fire burned down the downtown area of Spokane. In 1892 the railroad came to Spokane, and this made it easier for people to get into and out of the city. In 1974 Spokane hosted the World's Fair. This brought a lot of people and business into Spokane. ==Spokane River== File:Spokane Falls at Low Water - panoramio.jpg The Spokane River flows through the city. In downtown Spokane, there is a large waterfall called the Spokane Falls. The first buildings in Spokane were built near the falls.
