Subject: Dietary mineral
A '''dietary mineral''' is a chemical element that a living thing needs to be part of what they eat, other from the four elements carbon, hydrogen, nitrogen, and oxygen which are already very common in organic chemistry. One example of a dietary mineral is calcium, which humans and many other animals need to build strong bones and tooth, among other things. Iron is another mineral, which you can find in red meat or spinach. It is important for us to have iron as it creates Blood cell.
