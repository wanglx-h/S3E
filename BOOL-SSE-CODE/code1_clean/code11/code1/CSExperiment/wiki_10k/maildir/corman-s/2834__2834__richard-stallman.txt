File:Richard Stallman in Moscow, 2019 044.jpg '''Richard Stallman''' (born March 16, 1953) is the founder of the free software movement, the GNU project, and the Free Software Foundation. He is also a famous hacker. He created GNU Emacs, the GNU Compiler Collection, and the [https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/GNU_Debugger GNU Debugger]. He is one of the main authors of the GNU General Public License (''GNU GPL'' or ''GPL''), the most used free software license, which pioneered the concept of the copyleft. Since the mid-1990s, he has spent most of his time as a political campaigner, talking about free software and campaigning against proprietary software, software idea patents and expansions of copyright law. The time that he still spends on computer programming is spent on GNU Emacs.
