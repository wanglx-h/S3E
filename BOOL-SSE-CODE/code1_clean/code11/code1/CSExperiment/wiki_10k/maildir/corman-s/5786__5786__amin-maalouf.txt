| birth_place = Beirut, Lebanon | occupation = Writer, scholar and novelist | language = | notableworks = | website = }} '''Amin Maalouf''' (; ; born 25 February 1949) is a Lebanese author. He studied sociology at the French University in Beirut. He was the director of the Beirut daily newspaper ''an-Nahar''. But, he left the country 1975 at the start of the Lebanese Civil War. He moved to Paris and still lives there today. Maalouf writes in French language, and his works have been translated into many languages. He received the Prix Goncourt in 1993 for his book ''Rock of Tanios''. He uses his experiences of civil war and migration in writing his novels. The characters in his novels are wanderers.
