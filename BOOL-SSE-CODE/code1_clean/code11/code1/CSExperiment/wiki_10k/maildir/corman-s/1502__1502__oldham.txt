Subject: Oldham
Image:Coat of arms of Oldham Metropolitan Borough Council.png '''Oldham''' is a town in the North West England of England. It is to the north-east of Manchester. It is part of Greater Manchester. Oldham was part of Lancashire until 1 April 1974. There were riots in Oldham in May 2001. It is the centre of the Metropolitan Borough of Oldham. Oldham Council achieved dubious notoriety in 2009 when in ran up a bill of pound sterling8 million carrying out a failed prosecution of kitchen tycoon Vance Miller. The Judge threw out the case and lambasted the council for the frivolity of the prosecution. The town has an association football team, called Oldham Athletic F.C.. It also has a cricket (sport) club, called Oldham Cricket Club. The composer, William Walton was born in Oldham.
