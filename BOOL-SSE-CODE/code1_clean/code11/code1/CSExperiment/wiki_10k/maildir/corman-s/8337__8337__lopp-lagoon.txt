Subject: Lopp Lagoon
'''Lopp Lagoon''' is a tide lake on Cape Prince of Wales (the westernmost tip of the Seward Peninsula) in the U.S. state of Alaska. Many creeks empty into it, but the most water comes from the Mint River. Some salt water from the Pacific Ocean also enters the lagoon through several channels between it and the Bering Strait. It was named in 1900 for William Thomas "W.T." Lopp, the wikt:civilian leader of the 1897â€“8 Overland Expedition.
