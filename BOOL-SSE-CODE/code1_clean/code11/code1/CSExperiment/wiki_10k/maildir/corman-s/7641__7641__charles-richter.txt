File:CharlesRichter.jpg '''Charles Francis Richter''' (April 26, 1900 â€“ September 30, 1985) was an Americans seismologist (someone who studies earthquakes) and a physicist. He is known for creating the Richter scale of magnitude, which says how big an earthquake is. == Childhood == Richter went to work at the Carnegie Institution for Science in 1927 after Robert Millikan offered him a position as a research assistant there, where he began a collaboration with Beno Gutenberg. The Caltech Seismological Laboratory at the California Institute of Technology wanted to begin publishing regular reports on earthquakes in southern California and had a pressing need for a system of measuring the strength of earthquakes for these reports.
