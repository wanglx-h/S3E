Subject: Thyroid cancer
'''Thyroid cancer''' is cancer in the thyroid gland. Doctors can find thyroid cancer by checking for swelling in the neck, blood tests, special scans, or taking a biopsy. ==Types== There are four types of thyroid cancer: {| class="wikitable" style="margin: 1em auto 1em auto;" ! scope="col" style="width: 50px;" | '''Type of Thyroid Cancer:''' ! scope="col" style="width: 225px;" | '''This Type Makes Up...''' ! scope="col" style="width: 225px;" | '''Does it Grow Slowly or Quickly?''' ! scope="col" style="width: 225px;" | '''Can it metastasis (Spread)?''' ! scope="col" style="width: 225px;" | '''How Do People Do with Treatment?''' |- |Papillary |70% of all thyroid cancers |Slowly |Yes, but not quickly |People do well with treatment; patients under 45 rarely die from papillary thyroid cancer |- |Follicular |10-15% of all thyroid cancers |Slowly |Yes, through the Circulatory system and into other Organ (anatomy), like the lungs |People do well with treatment; patients under 45 rarely die from follicular thyroid cancer |- |Medullary |4% of all thyroid cancers |Usually slowly |Yes, early on |People do well if the cancer is only in the thyroid gland, but not as well if it has spread to other body parts |- |Anaplastic |2% of all thyroid cancers |Quickest of all thyroid cancers |Yes, very quickly | |} ==References== Category:Medicine Category:Cancer Category:Oncology Category:Diseases and disorders of the thyroid
