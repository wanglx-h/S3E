Subject: Platinum
'''Platinum''' is a chemical element. It has Symbol (chemistry) '''Pt''' and atomic number 78. Platinum is a wikt:soft, heavy, white metal. It is a precious metal and a transition metal. Its name comes from the Spanish language word meaning "little silver". Platinum is part of the platinum group of elements. It is on Group 10 element in the periodic table of elements. Its atoms have an atomic weight of 195 Atomic mass unit. It has 6 isotopes that is found in nature. Platinum can be found in some nickel and copper ores. 90% of platinum is found in the Ural Mountains, Colombia, the Sudbury Basin of Canada, and a large reserve in South Africa.File:Platinum nuggets.jpg In the past, it price more than gold. Since the late 2010s, gold cost more. The element is Abundance of elements in Earth's crust. It has an average abundance of about 5Microgram/kg. Since it is so rare, only a few hundred Tonne of Platinum are made every year. Platinum is very Density; almost twice as dense as lead. It is malleable and wikt:ductile. This means it can be made thin sheets and it can be pulled into wire. Platinum is very stable. Acids do not change platinum. Platinum has many uses. The most common use of Platinum is in a vehicle's catalytic converter. It is also used in laboratory equipment, Electric contacts and Electrode, Platinum resistance thermometer, dentistry equipment, and jewelry. Platinum is also used in the glass industry. There, it is used to change molten glass. Elemental platinum is not found to be dangerous to human health.
