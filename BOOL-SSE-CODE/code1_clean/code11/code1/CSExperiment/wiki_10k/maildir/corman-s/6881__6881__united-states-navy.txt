The '''United States Navy''' is part of the United States Armed Forces (the military of the United States). It is the largest navy in the world, with the estimated tonnage of its active battle fleet alone exceeding the next 13 navies combined, including 11 U.S. allies or partner nations. It has the highest combined battle fleet tonnage and the world's largest aircraft carrier fleet, with eleven in service, two new carriers under construction, and five other carriers planned. With 336,978 personnel on active duty and 101,583 in the Ready Reserve, the U.S. Navy is the third-largest U.S. military service branch in terms of personnel. The Department of the Navy needed places to make ships, which are called Shipbuilding, in various port cities. Where Slavery were available, it paid Slavery to rent their slaves. Shipyards with slaves included those of Washington, D.C.; Norfolk, Virginia; and Pensacola, Florida. == Sailors == Today, the U.S. Navy has over 300,000 Sailor and Officer, and almost another 100,000 workers who are not official sailors. == Strength == The U.S. Navy has about 300 Warship, ships designed for fighting. The largest warships are Aircraft carrier, which have large and flat decks that act as small airports for planes carrying weapons. The U.S. Navy has more of aircraft carriers than any other navy in the world and a large number of planes in naval aviation.
