Subject: Jean-Philippe Rameau
File:Attribué à Joseph Aved, Portrait de Jean-Philippe Rameau (vers 1728) - 001.jpg, 1728]] '''Jean-Philippe Rameau''' (born Dijon, September ? 1683; died Paris, 12 September 1764) was a French people composer. He is famous for his music for harpsichord and for his operas. His most well-known operas are: ''Hippolyte et Aricie'', ''Castor et Pollux'', and ''Dardanus''. He is also known for his one act opera "Pigmalion" which he is said to have composed in 8 days.
