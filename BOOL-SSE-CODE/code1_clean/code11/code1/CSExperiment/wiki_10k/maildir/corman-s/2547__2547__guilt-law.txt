Subject: Guilt (law)
In criminal law, a person is '''guilty''' if a Court (law) has decided they have done something illegal. For instance, if a person has broken a law by theft, he or she is guilty of a crime. A person is guilty if a court decides him or her to be, i.e. if the court Blame them for Wrongdoing. A guilty person is Punishment. The punishment is called ''Sentence (law)''.
