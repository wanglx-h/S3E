File:Dover from air.jpg File:Dover Port 2 (Piotr Kuczynski).jpg '''Dover''' is a town on the coast in Kent, in the United Kingdom. It was built by the Ancient Rome. There is a castle in Dover, called Dover Castle. It is the largest castle in England. The population is about 39,078. Throughout history, it has been an important Harbour of Great Britain because it is the closest port to mainland Europe. The sea between Dover and the France port of Calais is called the Strait of Dover. It is the narrowest part of the English Channel at only wide. The ferry port in Dover has ferries to Calais, France and Ostende, Belgium. Almost 45,000 people and 15,000 vehicles pass through the port every day. On of the largest Tourism around Dover are the White Cliffs of Dover. Dover District Council is responsible for checking food for diseases when it comes through the port. ==Dover History== The Romans first tried to invasion Dover in 55BC, under Julius Caesar. They were forced back by the Celts, who wikt:fierce defended the coastline. It was not until 43 AD, under the rule of List of Roman emperors Claudius, that Roman forces landed on British soil at Richborough and took control of the land as far as the northern border.
