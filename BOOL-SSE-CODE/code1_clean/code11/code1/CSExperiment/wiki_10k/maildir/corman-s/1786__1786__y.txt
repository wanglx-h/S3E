In words like ''year'', ''yell'', and ''yes'', the Y is a consonant (semivowel). In words like ''cry'', ''fly'', and ''sky'', the Y is considered a vowel. == Where it came from == ===Semitic, Phoenician, Greek and Latin=== Proto-semiticW-01.png|An early Semitic version of the letter waw (letter) Phoenician waw.svg|The later Phoenician version of waw "Y" has appeared as the Semitic letter "waw". This was the first time it appeared in an alphabet. F, U, V, and W also come from the Semitic alphabet. The Greek and Latin alphabets used the Phoenician form of this early alphabet. There are similarities to the old English letter yogh (Ȝȝ).
