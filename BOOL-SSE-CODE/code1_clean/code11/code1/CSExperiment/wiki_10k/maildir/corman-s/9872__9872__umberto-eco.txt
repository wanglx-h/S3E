His career as an author began with '':en:The_Name_of_the_Rose'' in 1980, after he had already written many academic papers. == Biography == Eco was born on January 5, 1932 in Alessandria. His family had 13 sons. He studied philosophy and humanities at the :en:University_of_Turin. He received a Doctor of Philosophy there. Eco worked as a professor in different places. Starting in 1971, he held the chair of semiotics at the University of Bologna. At a university, a "chair" is the highest wikt:rank a professor can earn. He was also given honorary degrees by thirty different universities. He had been named satrap of :en:'Pataphysics for his humourous works. One of his most important books is ''How to Travel with a Salmon.'' He was a member of UNESCO's UNESCO Council of Sages. In 2000, he received the :en:Princess_of_Asturias_Awards for Communications and Humanities. Eco worked in the mass media as well, creating culture programs. His interests were the Middle Ages, languages, and the classics. He was also an expert on James Bond. On February 19, 2016, Eco died at his home in Milan, Italy, of pancreatic cancer.
