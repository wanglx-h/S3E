The Honourable Excellency |residence = Bellevue Palace (Germany) (Berlin)Villa Hammerschmidt (Bonn) |appointer = Federal Convention (Germany) |termlength = Five years |termlength_qualified = Renewable once, consecutively |constituting_instrument = Basic Law for the Federal Republic of Germany |precursor = The President of Germany (1919–1945) |formation = 24 May 1949 |first = Theodor Heuss |website = }} The '''President of Germany''' (German language: ''Bundespräsident'', literally: ''federal president'') is the head of state of the Federal Republic of Germany. His functions are mostly supervising and representative, because of the negative experiences with the office of its predecessor the Reich President (''Reichskanzler''), who had more power than the current Federal President. The president is elected every five years by the Bundesversammlung.
