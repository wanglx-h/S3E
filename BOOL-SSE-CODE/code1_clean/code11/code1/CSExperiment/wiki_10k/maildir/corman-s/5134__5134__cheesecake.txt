File:Lemon Cheesecake Fontanella Tea Garden.jpg File:Maid cafe - Welcome at home cheesecake (2009-04-23 22.54.52 by panina.anna).jpg File:1963 Mandarin Cheese cake.jpg A '''cheesecake''' is a dessert cake made of cheese (usually cream cheese but sometimes ricotta), Egg (food)s, and sugar. It often has a graham cracker crumb crust, sometimes held together by melting butter. The filling has a very heavy, smooth texture. It sometimes has sour cream to make it easier to shape and may have fruit flavoring such as strawberry, blueberry, or lemon. It is often topping with fresh fruit or chocolate. == History == Cheesecake is now often thought to be part of American cuisine, but it has a long history. Cheese making began around 2nd millennium BC, and Archaeology have found cheese molds almost as old. Cheesecakes are known to go back to at least ancient Greece. Placenta cakes were the closest to today's cheesecakes, since its crust and filling are made separately. Europeans stopped using yeast and started adding egg beater Egg (food)s to their cheesecakes around the 18th century. This made it taste more like a dessert than a cheesebread.[https://www.cheesecake.com/History-Of-Cheesecake.asp "History of Cheesecake"]. Cheesecake recipes were brought to the United States as immigrants from Europe. Most of today's kinds of cheesecake ("New York City-style cheesecake") come from the kind created by William Lawrence of Chester, Orange County, New York, in 1872.
