|birth_place = Duncan, Oklahoma, USA |nationality = Americans |occupation = |years active = |party = Democratic |spouse = Cheryl Alley (m. 1975) |children = Bryce Dallas HowardPaige HowardJocelyn HowardReed Howard |relatives = Clint Howard (brother) |parents = Jean Speegle HowardRance Howard }} '''Ronald William Howard''' (born March 1, 1954) is an Americans actor, movie director, movie producer, and screenwriter. Ron Howard was born in Duncan, Oklahoma. His father is actor Rance Howard. He started acting as a young child. As an actor he was best known for his roles on television, as Opie Taylor on ''The Andy Griffith Show'' and Richie Cunningham on ''Happy Days''. He appeared in movies like ''The Music Man'' (1962) and ''American Graffiti'' (1973). He later quit acting and became a director. Some of the movies he has directed are: ''Splash (movie)'' (1984), ''Cocoon (movie)'' (1985), ''Willow (movie)'' (1988), ''Parenthood (1989 movie)'' (1989), ''Apollo 13'' (1995), ''How the Grinch Stole Christmas'' (2000), ''A Beautiful Mind'' (2001), and ''Cinderella Man'' (2005). His father Rance Howard, brother Clint Howard and daughters Bryce Dallas Howard and Paige Howard are also actors, as was his mother, Jean Speegle Howard.
