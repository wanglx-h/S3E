Subject: Gaia
'''Gaia''' (), also spelled '''Gaea''', is the personification of the Earth in Greek mythology. She was a primordial being, one of the first to have sprung forth from the void of Chaos (mythology). She is the mother and wife of Uranos (Father Sky), with whom she sired the Titan (mythology), the Hekatonkheires, and the Cyclops. Other children of Gaia include Pontus (mythology) (the sea), and the storm giant Typhon. Her Roman mythology equivalent is Terra (mythology).''Larousse Desk Reference Encyclopedia'', The Book People, Haydock, 1995, p. 215. ==Family tree== Gaia is the goddess of the Earth and these are her offspring as related in various myths.
