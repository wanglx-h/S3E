| birth_place = Rosario, Santa Fe Province, Argentina | death_date = | death_place = La Higuera, Vallegrande Province, Bolivia }} '''Ernesto "Che" Guevara''', commonly known as '''el Che''' or '''Che''' (June 14, 1928 - October 9, 1967) was an Argentine Marxism, revolutionary, physician, author, Guerrilla warfare, diplomat, and military theorist. He is popular with socialists. In addition to that he was a writer and some of his articles and speeches were even published. Time (magazine) said he was one of the most influence people of the 20th century. Guevara's face, with his thick black hair, scruffy beard and familiar beret, has been printed on T-shirts, walls and banners.https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/retropolis/wp/2017/10/09/do-not-shoot-the-last-moments-of-communist-revolutionary-che-guevara/ ''Guerrillero Heroico, ''a picture of him, is called "the most famous photograph in the world."Maryland Institute of Art, referenced at BBC News, "Che Guevara photographer dies", 26 May 2001. [http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/world/americas/1352650.stm Online at BBC News], accessed January 4, 2006. == Biography == Che Guevara was born in Rosario, Argentina in 1928. He studied at the University of Buenos Aires. He wanted to be a doctor. During his holidays, he traveled. He saw how poor some people were. He thought that revolution could fix it. After college, he went to Mexico. He met Fidel Castro there in 1954. Castro was planning a rebellion in Cuba. Guevara joined Castro. He fought with Castro in Cuba. In 1959, Fidel won and became president of Cuba. Fidel and Guevara declared Cuba a Communism country. Guevara was made head of Cuba's bank. He later became Minister of Industry. In 1965, Guevara helped to start other revolutions. They were in Democratic Republic of the Congo and Bolivia. In 1967, he was captured and executed by Bolivian soldiers. His body was not found until 1997. His nickname among friends was "Chegi Vara". == Che in popular culture == File:Che por Jim Fitzpatrick.svg People have different opinions on Che Guevara.
