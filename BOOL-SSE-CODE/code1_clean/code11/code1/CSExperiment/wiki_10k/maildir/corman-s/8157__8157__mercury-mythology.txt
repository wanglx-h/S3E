Subject: Mercury (mythology)
File:Mercurius.jpg '''Mercury''' (Latin: ''Mercurius'') is the god of trade, commerce, financial gain, messages/communication, travellers and boundaries, trickery, merchants and thieves in Ancient Roman religion and Roman mythology. Son of Jupiter (mythology) and Maia, Mercury was a major god in Roman state religion, being a member of the Dii Consentes, the principal group of ancient Roman deities. The Roman counterpart to Hermes, the god is depicted as holding a caduceus in his left hand. The planet Mercury (planet) and the element :en:Mercury_(element) are named after him. ==Related pages== *:en:Turms - Etruscan mythology version of Mercury. * Hermes - Greek mythology version of Mercury.
