| blank2_name = Public transit | blank2_info = | website = | named_for = William Fargo }} '''Fargo''' is the is the county seat of Cass County, North Dakota. The city was founded in 1871, and it has a population of 125,990 as of the 2020 census. This makes it the largest city in the United States state of North Dakota. It is the List of United States cities by population in America. Fargo is in the eastern part of the state, near the Minnesota border. Fargo is at the Intersection (road) of two major Highway, Interstate 29 and Interstate 94. Fargo has a Twin cities of Moorhead, Minnesota. Fargo, Moorhead, West Fargo, North Dakota, and Dilworth, Minnesota, are part of the Fargo-Moorhead Metropolitan statistical area. Fargo was founded in 1871 on the Red River of the North floodplain. Fargo is very important to North Dakota. It is a Culture, retail, Healthcare, education, and industry center in the Midwestern United States. North Dakota State University is in the city. == History == Fargo is on the Red River of the North. Fargo was originally part of Lake Agassiz. When the lake drained, it formed the Red River Valley. Many Native American groups lived near this area. By the 19th century, Fargo was part of Sioux (Dakota people) territory. By 1859, there were many settlements in the Red River Valley. The area that is now Fargo was a stopping point for Steamboat traveling across the Red River of the North. In 1871, Centralia was established. However, the town was soon renamed to Fargo, after William Fargo. While Fargo was being developed, two separate towns started to form: "Fargo on the Prairie" and "Fargo on the Timber". Fargo on the Prairie was a tent town. It was said to be well-lead by both Northern Pacific Railway engineers and military officers. Fargo on the Timber was known for having lots of whisky and being lawless. In 1872, federal troops shut down Fargo on the Timber, leaving only Fargo on the Prairie.
