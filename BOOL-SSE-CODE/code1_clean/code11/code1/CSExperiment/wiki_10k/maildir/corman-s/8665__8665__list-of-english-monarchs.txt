Subject: List of English monarchs
The King of England was the supreme head of state and head of government of the Kingdom of England. This is a list of the '''Kings and Queens of the England''' from 924 until England and Scotland joined together in 1707. == First kings == * Athelstan (927–939) * Edmund I of England (939–946) * Edred of England (946–955) * Edwy of England (955–959) * Edgar of England (959–975) * Edward the Martyr (975–978) * Ethelred the Unready (978–1013, 1014–1016) * Sweyn I of Denmark (1013–1014) ''(from Denmark)'' * Edmund II of England (1016) == Danish kings == * Cnut the Great (1016–1035) * Harold Harefoot (1037–1040) * Harthacanute (1035–1037, 1040–1042) == Last Anglo-Saxon kings == House of Wessex * Edward the Confessor (1042–1066) House of Godwin * Harold Godwinson (1066) == Normans == In 1066, William, Duke of Normandy invaded England. He defeated King Harold Godwinson and became King. * William I of England (1066–1087) * William II of England (1087–1100) * Henry I of England (1100–1135) House of Blois * Stephen of England (1135–1154) == Plantagenets == === Angevins === File:King Edward III from NPG.jpg * Empress Matilda (1141) * Henry II of England (1154–1189) * Richard I of England (1189–1199) * John I of England (1199–1216) * Henry III of England (1216–1272) * Edward I of England (1272–1307) * Edward II of England (1307–1327) (deposed) * Edward III of England (1327–1377) * Richard II of England (1377–1399) (deposed, died 1400) === Lancastrians === * Henry IV of England (1399–1413) * Henry V of England (1413–1422) * Henry VI of England (1422–1461 and 1470–1471) === Yorkists === * Edward IV of England (1461–1470 and 1471–1483) * Edward V of England (uncrowned) (1483) (deposed 1483 possibly assassinated) * Richard III of England (1483–1485) == Tudors == The House of Tudor were from Wales. In 1536, Wales became part of England. England had controlled Wales since 1284. * Henry VII of England (1485–1509) * Henry VIII of England (1509–1547) * Edward VI of England (1547–1553) * Lady Jane Grey (uncrowned) (1553) (deposed, beheaded 1554) * Mary I of England (1553–1558) * Elizabeth I of England (1558–1603) == Stuarts == The House of Stuart were also Scottish monarchs, with which kingdom England was in Personal union, but not legal union until 1707. * James I of England (1603–1625), also from 1567 King James VI of Scotland * Charles I of England (1625–1649), also King of Scotland == Interregnum == The Civil War in England from 1642 until 1652 stemming from a growing enmity between King and Parliament, led to the execution of King Charles I in 1649. After the execution, England became a Commonwealth of England eventually led by Oliver Cromwell as Lord Protector after successive interim governments failed and handed Cromwell power, and so England became a protectorate.''The Cromwellian Protectorate'', ed. Barry Coward (Manchester: Manchester University Press, 2002), p. 7 Furthermore, both Ireland and Scotland became subjugated states under England and Cromwell at the end of the war. Cromwell died in 1658 and his son, Richard, became Lord Protector. This was short lived though as he failed to gain the support of the army and so the nation, in 1660 power was given back to the Monarchy and the King In Exile, Charles II, was invited back to EnglandJacob Abbott, ''History of King Charles II of England'' (Auckland: The Floating Press, 2014), p. 124 == Stuarts (restored) == * Charles II of England (1660–1685), also King of Scotland (backdated the start of his reign to 1649) * James II of England (1685–1688) (deposed, died 1701), also King James VII of Scotland * William III of England (1689–1702) and Mary II of England (1689–1694), as co-monarchs, also King and Queen of Scotland * Anne of Great Britain (1702–1714), though the English throne was replaced with that of the United Kingdom of Great Britain in 1707 == Longest To Shortest Reigning == * James VI and I (57 Years) * Henry III of England (56 Years) * Edward III of England (50 Years) * Elizabeth I (44 Years) * Henry VI of England (1st Reign 40 Years) * Henry VIII (38 Years) ==Related pages== *In 1707, England and Scotland joined together.
