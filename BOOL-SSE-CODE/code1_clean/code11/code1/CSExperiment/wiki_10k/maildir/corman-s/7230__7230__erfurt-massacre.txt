Image:GutenbergGymErfurt.JPG Image:Gutenberg-gymnasium-erfurt september 2005 005.jpg The '''Erfurt massacre''' was a mass shooting that happened at the Gutenberg Gymnasium (school) in Erfurt, Thuringia, Germany, on 26 April 2002. A boy that had been expulsion (education) from the school 2 months earlier, 19-year-old Robert Steinhäuser, was the person who did all the shooting. In anger, he came into the gymnasium, and shot dead 12 teachers, a secretary, two students, and a police officer. After a teacher caught him and put him in an empty room, Steinhäuser suicide. In total, 17 people were killed.
