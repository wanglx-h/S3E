Subject: Petula Clark
File:Petula Clark April 2012.jpg '''Petula Clark''', Order of the British Empire (born 15 November 1932) is a British people singer, actress and composer. She was born in Epsom, Surrey. She began her career as a child and was popular in Britain during World War II. In the 1950s, she became a successful pop music singer, first in the UK, then in continental Europe, and finally in the United States in the mid-1960s. Her most famous song is "Downtown (song)," which was recorded in four languages and sold nearly five million copies worldwide. Her films include ''The Card'' (1952), ''The Runaway Bus'' (1956), ''Finian's Rainbow'' (1968), and ''Goodbye, Mr. Chips''.
